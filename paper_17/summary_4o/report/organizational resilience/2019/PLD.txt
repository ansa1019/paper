Our operations and structure involve various risks that could adversely affect our business and financial condition, including social, political, and economic risks of doing business in many countries, which may impact our organizational resilience. Our ability to attract and retain executive officers is crucial for maintaining our competitive position and organizational health. Deficiencies in our internal control over financial reporting could result in misstatements or restatements of our financial statements, impacting our performance under pressure. We are exposed to foreign currency risk due to potential fluctuations in exchange rates, which may have a material adverse effect on our financial position and results of operations. Our investments are concentrated in the logistics sector, which may expose us to economic downturns in that sector, affecting our operational flexibility and adaptability. We evaluate our earthquake insurance coverage annually, which is part of our risk management strategy to mitigate potential hazards. Our real estate development strategies may not be successful, and we may not be able to attract third-party investment or sufficient customer demand, impacting our recovery and resilience strategies. We are subject to risks and liabilities in connection with forming co-investment ventures, which may affect our ability to maintain relationships with customers and achieve anticipated benefits. The integration of operations from acquisitions may result in distractions or disruptions that could adversely affect our organizational effectiveness. Our liquidity and capital resources are essential to meet anticipated future development, acquisition, and operational needs, and we expect to fund our cash needs from various sources, including net cash flow from property operations and proceeds from property dispositions. We are subject to risks associated with our dependence on key personnel and the potential adverse effects of changes in their roles. Our organizational practices and strategic initiatives are designed to enhance our resilience and adaptability in the face of external pressures and environmental disruptions. We continuously assess our performance and operational resources to ensure we can respond effectively to unexpected situations and maintain our organizational coherence. Our ability to manage debt maturities and access capital markets is crucial for our future growth and organizational survival, particularly in turbulent markets.
