The Coca-Cola Company recognizes that its success depends on its ability to connect with consumers by providing a wide variety of beverage choices to meet their desires, needs, and lifestyle choices. The company’s objective is to use its formidable assets—brands, financial strength, unrivaled distribution system, global reach, and the talent and strong commitment of its management and associates—to achieve long-term sustainable growth. The company operates in a highly competitive nonalcoholic beverage segment, facing strong competition from numerous other beverage companies, which necessitates continuous strengthening of capabilities in marketing and innovation to maintain brand loyalty and market share. The company is committed to meeting evolving consumer preferences by generating new growth through a diverse portfolio of over 500 brands and more than 4,100 beverage products, including nearly 1,300 low- and no-calorie products. The company emphasizes the importance of innovation in addressing public health challenges such as obesity and is committed to offering reduced-, low-, or no-calorie beverage options while providing transparent nutrition information. Water quality and quantity are critical issues that require sustained attention and collaboration with various stakeholders, including suppliers and local communities. The company has implemented a formal water risk management program, which is part of its water stewardship strategic framework, aimed at addressing local needs and ensuring the sustainability of this vital resource. The company also recognizes the impact of external pressures, such as economic conditions and consumer preferences, on its operations and is focused on resource allocation and operational flexibility to adapt to these changes. The company’s strategic priorities include accelerating growth of a consumer-centric brand portfolio, driving revenue growth, strengthening the Coca-Cola system, digitizing the enterprise, and unlocking the power of its people. The company is dedicated to enhancing its core capabilities in consumer marketing, commercial leadership, and franchise leadership to support these strategic initiatives. The company’s productivity and reinvestment program aims to drive long-term profitable growth through operational excellence and increased marketing effectiveness. The company regularly assesses risks related to various economic, political, and regulatory environments, particularly in developing and emerging markets, to ensure the recoverability of its assets. The company’s organizational structure is designed to support its operational management and strategic response to challenges, enabling it to maintain performance under pressure and adapt to complex situations. The company’s approach to crisis management includes preparing for unexpected situations and ensuring operational continuity. The company believes that its current liquidity position is strong, allowing it to meet financial commitments and continue investing in its growth and resilience strategies.
