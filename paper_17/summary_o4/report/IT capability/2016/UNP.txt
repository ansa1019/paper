The company discloses that it relies on information technology in all aspects of its business and depends on having sufficient capital to acquire, develop and implement new technologies—such as positive train control and the latest transportation control systems—to support network operations and maintain a competitive position; it warns that failure to do so may result in competitive disadvantage and that a cyber attack or other event causing significant disruption or failure of one or more of its IT systems, including computer hardware, software and communications equipment, could trigger service interruptions, safety failures, security breaches or other operational difficulties with material adverse effects on its results of operations, financial condition and liquidity. In its 2017 outlook the company states it will continue deploying technology, risk assessment, quality control measures and targeted capital investments to enhance safety and operational efficiency, and its 2017 capital plan includes approximately $300 million dedicated to positive train control and ongoing technology improvements as part of its overall cash capital investments.
