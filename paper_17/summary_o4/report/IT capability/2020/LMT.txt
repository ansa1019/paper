Lockheed Martin’s annual report notes “software development” as a key challenge within the F-35 program, alongside supplier performance and sustainment costs. Its Rotary and Mission Systems segment describes offerings that include “command and control mission solutions, cyber solutions, and simulation and training solutions,” reflecting the integration of advanced digital and cyber capabilities. The company also reports capital expenditures “for information technology to support programs and general enterprise information technology infrastructure, inclusive of costs for the development or purchase of internal-use software,” demonstrating ongoing investment in enterprise IT resources. Finally, the report records a non-cash charge in discontinued operations related to tax matters of the former Information Systems & Global Solutions business, underscoring the scope and impact of its historical information systems activities.
