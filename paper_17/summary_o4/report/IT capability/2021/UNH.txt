UnitedHealth Group acknowledges that “the ongoing COVID-19 pandemic continues to impact health systems, businesses, governments and customer and consumer activities” and describes its organizational resilience as the capacity to absorb strain and preserve or improve functioning despite public health crises, supply-chain disruptions, regulatory changes and economic volatility.  Its resilience strategies include forecasting, pricing for and managing medical costs through underwriting criteria, product design, competitive provider contracting and care management programs; continuously consolidating, integrating, upgrading and expanding its information systems “to keep pace with continuing changes in information processing technology, evolving systems and regulatory standards and changing customer preferences”; an “ongoing commitment of significant resources to maintain, protect and enhance existing systems and develop new systems”; programs intended to detect, contain and respond to cyber-security incidents and to provide employee awareness training regarding phishing, malware and other risks; productivity and cost management initiatives across Optum and UnitedHealthcare to mitigate the impacts of reduced demand, temporary care deferrals and pandemic-related care costs; rigorous completion factor and medical cost-per-member trend analyses with sensitivity testing to manage reserve development; annual and event-driven goodwill impairment assessments using discounted cash flows and market multiples; inter-organizational collaboration via value-based arrangements, network design, strategic alliances and acquisitions subject to effective integration; talent attraction, development, retention and succession planning for key employees and executives; and diversified financing from operating cash flows, dividends from regulated subsidiaries, a committed commercial paper program backed by revolving bank credit facilities and periodic long-term debt issuances.  Through these measures, the company seeks to maintain liquidity and financial flexibility, ensure operational continuity, adapt to environmental perturbations, mitigate risks, support business continuity and transform itself to emerge stronger under challenging conditions.
