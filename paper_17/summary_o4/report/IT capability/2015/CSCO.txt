Our Form 10-K emphasizes that our operating results depend on our ability to develop and introduce new products into existing and emerging markets and to reduce the production costs of existing products; that many of our strategic initiatives and investments are aimed at meeting the requirements for networks capable of multiple-party, collaborative interaction and that our architectural approach is designed to enable the network as the platform for all forms of communications and IT; that we launched the Cisco Unified Computing System as a next-generation enterprise data center platform architected to unite computing, networking, storage access and virtualization resources in a single system; that we are focusing on the move toward programmable, flexible, and virtual networks, on the Internet of Everything market transition, and on architecting the Cisco Intercloud solution; that we have transformed our business to move from selling individual products and services to integrated architectures and solutions and have reorganized how we build and deliver our technology; that our strategy is to lead customers in their digital transition with pervasive, industry-leading security and infrastructure that intelligently connects nearly everything; that together with our ecosystem of partners and developers we provide technology, services, and solutions to enable customers to gain insight and advantage from highly distributed data with scale, security, and agility; that we intend to continue investing in engineering, sales, service, and marketing functions dedicated to key growth areas such as data center virtualization, software, security, and cloud while maintaining leadership in routing, switching, and services; that our evolving business models—including offerings such as “Other-as-a-Service,” subscription-based revenue arrangements, and software licensing that tailors access, security, and connectivity to changing business needs—underscore our competence in mobilizing and deploying IT resources in support of business strategies and work processes; and that factors such as reduced IT-related capital spending, trust and confidence in us as an IT supplier, our ability to execute digital transformations, maintain operational flexibility, and manage our supply chain effectively may materially affect our firm performance.
