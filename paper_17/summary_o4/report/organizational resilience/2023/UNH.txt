The report describes the company’s establishment of business continuation and resiliency plans, maintained, updated and tested regularly to contain and remediate potential disruptions or cyber events, complemented by programs to detect, contain and respond to data security incidents and employee training on phishing, malware and other cyber threats; it details risk mitigation processes including underwriting criteria, product design, negotiation of competitive provider contracts and care management programs to manage medical cost volatility and the use of completion and trend factors to estimate incurred but not reported claims; it underscores continuous consolidation, integration, upgrade and expansion of information systems, ongoing investment in fast-evolving AI/ML technologies and development of metrics to monitor operations, care delivery and cost management, all intended to absorb internal and external pressures while preserving system integrity and performance; and it highlights the ongoing forecasting and management of cash, investments, working capital and capital structure—supported by revolving bank credit facilities, a commercial paper program and covenant compliance—to meet both short-term and long-term obligations, maintain liquidity and financial flexibility and ensure sufficient capital resources to support operational continuity and adaptability under adverse conditions.
