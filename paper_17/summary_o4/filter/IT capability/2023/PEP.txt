In “Item 1a. Risk Factors,” the report acknowledges that “our business operations, including our supply chain, are subject to disruption by geopolitical events, wars and other military conflicts, natural disasters, pandemics, epidemics or other events beyond our control that could negatively impact product availability and decrease demand for our products if our crisis management plans do not effectively mitigate these issues,” reflecting its reliance on strategic initiatives and crisis management protocols to preserve continuity under adverse conditions. The discussion of “failure to attract, develop and maintain a highly skilled and diverse workforce” notes that any “unplanned turnover, sustained labor shortage or unsuccessful implementation of our succession plans…can deplete our institutional knowledge base,” underscoring the importance of human capital and knowledge absorption in maintaining organizational health. Among commodity risks, the company states that when “input prices increase unexpectedly or significantly, we may be unwilling or unable to increase our product prices…without suffering reduced volume, revenue, margins and operating results,” highlighting its resource allocation and risk mitigation practices. In addressing retail disruption, the report explains that “we continue to monitor changes in the retail landscape and seek to identify actions we may take to build our global e-commerce and digital capabilities…to potentially mitigate any unfavorable impacts on our future results,” illustrating adaptation strategies and future orientation. The disclosure of its integrated risk management framework describes how “to identify, assess, prioritize, address, manage, monitor and communicate these risks across the company’s operations…and foster a corporate culture of integrity and risk awareness,” the company employs “division and key market risk committees” and a “PepsiCo Risk Committee” tasked with reporting to the board, thereby embedding a systemic perspective and hierarchical stakeholder engagement in its resilience strategy. The financing section further states that “we believe that our cash generating capability and financial condition, together with our revolving credit facilities…will be adequate to meet our operating, investing and financing needs,” portraying financial resourcefulness and operational flexibility. The management’s discussion of productivity and efficiency initiatives underscores “productivity initiatives that we believe will position our business for long-term sustainable growth by allowing us to achieve a lower cost structure, improve decision-making and operate more efficiently,” denoting a focus on dynamic capabilities and strategic adaptation. Through its pep+ transformation, the report elaborates that “to meet the challenges of today—and those of tomorrow—we are driven by an approach called pep+,” which spans “transforming our business operations, from sourcing ingredients and making and selling products in a more sustainable way, to driving positive change across our value chain,” reflecting a multifaceted resilience engineering approach designed to preserve organizational integrity and performance under environmental turbulence.
