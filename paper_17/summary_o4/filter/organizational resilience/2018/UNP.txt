Throughout the report, we articulate how we manage fluctuating demand for our services and network capacity—resizing our operations, adjusting workforce levels, and incurring higher unit operating costs and storage costs—to preserve service levels and financial stability under internal or external variations and disturbances; we continue to improve our transportation plan, add capacity, improve operations at our yards and other facilities, and improve our ability to address surges in demand for any reason with adequate resources, and we have implemented our unified plan 2020 with initiatives focused on increasing reliability of our service product, reducing variability in network operations, and improving resource utilization costs; we rely on information technology in all aspects of our business, implement positive train control and bolster cybersecurity protections to mitigate operational risks and safety failures; operating a safe railroad benefits all our constituents, and we deploy programs such as courage to care, total safety culture, and UP Way (our continuous improvement culture) to identify and implement best practices for employee and public safety; our capital plan includes expenditures to renew and improve our existing infrastructure as well as new capacity investments designed to support future business growth and operational efficiency, including locomotive modernization and freight cars; we maintain adequate resources, including our credit facility, receivables securitization facility, and access to capital markets to meet any foreseeable cash requirements and manage fuel price volatility through fuel surcharges; we perform environmental assessments and accrue remediation costs where obligations are probable and estimable; although climate change could have an adverse impact on our operations and financial performance, we continue to take steps and explore opportunities to reduce the impact of our operations on the environment, including investments in new technologies, using training programs to reduce fuel consumption, and changing our operations to increase fuel efficiency; we evaluate pension and other postretirement benefit obligations through actuarial analyses using discount rates and expected returns on plan assets; and we engage in labor negotiations under the Railway Labor Act to minimize the likelihood of strikes, all of which reflect our organizational flexibility, risk assessment practices, resource reconfiguration processes, and dynamic capabilities to absorb strain and preserve functioning under challenging conditions.
