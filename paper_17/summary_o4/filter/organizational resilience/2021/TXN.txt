The Company describes a range of operational, financial and strategic measures that support its ability to absorb strain and preserve or improve functioning despite adversity, including the modification of business practices, supply-chain operations and manufacturing forecasts in response to the COVID-19 pandemic, government actions and third-party disruptions; the deployment of resources and emergency management procedures to address short- or long-term constrained supply, volatility in customer demand and risks from domestic and international political, social, economic, health and climate-related events; the use of robust liquidity sources—cash flows from operations, cash and cash equivalents, short-term investments and access to revolving credit and debt markets—to fund working capital needs, capital expenditures, debt-related payments and shareholder returns; the undertaking of strategic, business and organizational changes, including acquisitions, divestitures, restructuring actions and significant investments in research and development and manufacturing capacity to strengthen supply-chain control, operational flexibility and long-term free cash flow per share; the application of risk management processes, inventory valuation allowances and tax provisioning to assess and mitigate exposure to trade tensions, cybersecurity events, natural disasters, competitive and pricing pressures, technological change and regulatory developments; and the continued focus on sustaining competitive advantages through disciplined capital allocation, strategic foresight and proactive adaptations that together serve to maintain system integrity, performance and continuity under varied internal and external disturbances.
