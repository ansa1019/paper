The report acknowledges that technological advancements and innovation are occurring at a rapid pace in distribution, underwriting, recordkeeping, advisory, claims and operations and that other companies’ increasing use of data analytics and technology as part of their business strategy could require our insurance business to incur additional costs to maintain its competitive position; it recognizes our heavy reliance on financial, accounting and other data processing systems and on third-party service providers for key operational functions and warns that failures, errors or interruptions—such as errors in execution, confirmation or settlement of transactions or reliance on manual processes—could materially and adversely affect our ability to continue to operate without interruption; it cautions that our information systems and technology infrastructure may not be able to accommodate growth, may be subject to security risks, may not be suitable for new products and strategies, and that the cost of maintaining or upgrading such systems may increase; it highlights heightened cybersecurity risks, including unauthorized access, theft or destruction of data, malware or denial-of-service attacks, and the challenge of protecting proprietary, confidential and sensitive information in ordinary operations and through third-party vendors; it notes that global atlantic depends on its third-party administrators and distribution partners for policy administration and client services, and that failures or disruptions on their part could impair service and harm our reputation; it underscores the impact of evolving global data privacy, cyber-security and protection laws—including the GDPR, CCPA/CPRA and proposed SEC cybersecurity rules—and the difficulty and cost of complying with these regulations, which may increase operational and compliance burdens and expose us to enforcement actions and reputational damage; and it recognizes that significant system, controls and personnel dependencies create operational risks that, if not mitigated through adequate risk management, could result in material adverse effects on our businesses, financial condition and results of operations.
