Occidental's strategy focuses on delivering a unique shareholder value proposition through continual enhancements to its asset quality, organizational capability, and innovative technical applications that provide competitive advantages. The oil and gas segment's operations benefit from scale, technical expertise, environmental and safety leadership, and commercial and governmental collaboration, which allow Occidental to bring additional production quickly to market and provide low-cost growth opportunities with advanced technology. Occidental aims to maximize shareholder returns through a combination of value growth that meets above cost-of-capital returns and return targets, while conducting operations with a focus on social responsibility commitments and initiatives. The company seeks to limit its financial and political risks and positions itself to thrive in varying commodity price environments. Recent strategic initiatives have resulted in Occidental exiting non-core areas and strengthening its position in areas where it has a competitive advantage. The oil and gas segment focuses on long-term value creation and leadership in health, safety, and the environment, with a disciplined approach to capital expenditures emphasizing returns and value creation. Occidental's capital expenditures reflect its strategy to focus on achieving returns above the cost of capital, even in low-price environments, with significant investments in the Permian Basin and international projects. The chemical segment's strategy is to be a low-cost producer to maximize cash flow generation, concentrating on the chlorovinyls chain to take advantage of economies of scale. The midstream and marketing segment strives to maximize realized value by optimizing the use of its transportation, storage, and terminal assets, providing access to domestic and international market alternatives. Occidental's investments in pipeline and gathering systems, gas plants, and storage facilities are aimed at improving the competitiveness of its businesses. The company evaluates opportunities across the value chain and uses its assets to provide services to subsidiaries and third parties. Occidental's operations are characterized by the application of physical and intangible IT resources such as technology, knowledge, practices, management skills, and business process understanding, which further organizational goals. The firm’s IT capabilities represent the competence to effectively support business needs, underscoring the importance of mobilizing and deploying IT-based resources in combination with leveraging the value of other resources and capabilities. Occidental's strategic positioning allows it to achieve low development and operating costs, promoting innovative ideas that differentiate its approach and provide future opportunities. The company’s ability to add reserves is dependent on the success of improved recovery, extension, and discovery projects, which rely on technology improvements and market conditions. Occidental's focus on operational excellence, cost-reduction efficiencies, and improvement in productivity is aimed at enhancing its overall performance and sustaining its competitive edge in the market.
