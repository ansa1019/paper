During fiscal 2018, we reported net sales of $108.2 billion, with online sales representing 7.9% of net sales and growing 26.2%. The increase in net sales was primarily driven by positive comparable sales, which reflected the execution of our strategic efforts to drive an enhanced interconnected experience in both the physical and digital worlds. Our comparable sales increased 5.2%, supported by strong sales in big ticket purchases in categories such as appliances and flooring. The adoption of ASU No. 2014-09 benefited net sales by $216 million in fiscal 2018, indicating our capability to adapt to regulatory changes and leverage technology for business advantage. We generated $13.0 billion of cash flow from operations, which was utilized for share repurchases, dividends, and capital expenditures, demonstrating effective resource allocation and management capability. Our capital expenditures for investments in the business amounted to $2.4 billion in fiscal 2018, reflecting our commitment to enhancing operational efficiency and supporting business innovation. The increase in gross profit margin was driven by a benefit from the adoption of ASU No. 2014-09 and a favorable mix of products sold, indicating our ability to leverage technology and operational processes for improved financial performance. We also recognized a $247 million impairment loss related to certain trade names associated with Interline, highlighting the challenges in managing our technology portfolio and brand assets. Our strategic resource allocation decisions, including a $15.0 billion share repurchase program authorized in February 2019, reflect our focus on enhancing shareholder value while maintaining operational flexibility. The effective tax rate of 23.6% for fiscal 2018 was lower than previous years, primarily due to the enactment of the tax act, which positively impacted our net earnings and cash flow. Our ability to manage working capital effectively contributed to an increase in cash flow from operations, emphasizing the importance of information management and operational agility in responding to market dynamics. The increase in comparable customer transactions and average ticket size during fiscal 2018 illustrates our capability to meet consumer demands and adapt to changing market conditions. Our investments in digital technologies and infrastructure are aimed at enhancing customer relationships and improving our competitive positioning in the market. The strategic decisions made regarding our technology capabilities and operational processes are integral to our overall business strategy and performance outcomes. The ongoing evolution of our IT capabilities underscores the importance of mobilizing and deploying IT-based resources in combination with leveraging other strategic resources to support our organizational goals and enhance firm performance.
