The Goldman Sachs Group, Inc. recognizes that information technology (IT) capabilities have been evolving in scope to encompass the coordination of a firm’s assets and internal processes and their interfirm transactions and relationships. IT capability is crucial for acquiring, deploying, combining, and reconfiguring IT resources to support and enhance business strategies and work processes. The firm emphasizes the importance of mobilizing and deploying IT-based resources in combination with leveraging the value of other resources and capabilities, which has become key in transforming the firm into a competitive force. The ability to provide data and information to users with appropriate levels of accuracy, timeliness, reliability, security, confidentiality, connectivity, and access is critical, as is the capability to tailor these in response to changing business needs and directions. The firm’s operational risk management framework integrates technology risk management, which includes cybersecurity risk, to mitigate potential threats to its data and systems. The firm has established a comprehensive governance structure to oversee its risk management policies, including those related to technology and operational resilience. The firmwide operational risk and resilience committee is responsible for overseeing operational risk and ensuring that critical functions can continue to operate in the event of disruptions. Additionally, the firm monitors and assesses third-party risks associated with its reliance on external service providers, including those related to technology. The firm’s investment in technology infrastructure supports its operational efficiency and enhances its capability to respond to market dynamics and competitive actions. The firm’s approach to managing liquidity risk involves maintaining a diversified funding profile and ensuring that its technology systems provide complete, accurate, and timely information for effective decision-making. The firm also employs various risk measures, including value-at-risk (VaR) and earnings-at-risk (EaR), to monitor market risk and ensure compliance with established limits. The integration of climate-related risks into the firm’s risk management framework reflects the evolving nature of operational and strategic risks, emphasizing the need for adaptability in its technology and operational processes. The firm’s commitment to maintaining a robust cybersecurity program is critical in safeguarding its information systems and ensuring business continuity. The firm’s technology capabilities are essential for enhancing its competitive advantage, enabling operational flexibility, and supporting strategic resource allocation. The firm’s ability to leverage big data and analytics contributes to its market responsiveness and enhances its overall performance. The strategic alignment of technology resources with business objectives is fundamental to the firm’s ongoing transformation and success in a rapidly changing financial landscape.
