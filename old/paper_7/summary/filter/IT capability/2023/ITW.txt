The ITW business model is the company's core source of value creation and defines how ITW creates value for its shareholders. The 80/20 front-to-back process is the operating system applied in every ITW business, generating significant value through data-driven insights that focus on the largest opportunities while eliminating cost and complexity associated with less profitable ones. This model enables ITW to achieve world-class operational excellence in product availability, quality, and innovation, contributing to superior financial performance. Customer-back innovation, driven by insights from the 80/20 process, positions ITW as a problem solver for key customers, fostering a culture of innovation that has led to a substantial portfolio of patents. The decentralized, entrepreneurial culture of ITW allows businesses to be fast, focused, and responsive, providing significant flexibility to customize approaches to meet specific customer needs. The strategic framework initiated in 2012 aims to leverage the capabilities and operating practices of the ITW business model, focusing on consistent above-market growth and best-in-class margins. Key initiatives include portfolio management, business structure simplification, and strategic sourcing, which have established sourcing as a core operational capability. The application of the 80/20 process optimizes scaled-up divisions for growth, building a foundation of operational excellence and identifying opportunities for organic growth. The company maintains a focus on sustainable differentiation and evaluates its portfolio to ensure it delivers long-term performance. The strategic sourcing initiative has resulted in an average reduction in spend, contributing to the enterprise strategy. The ability to consistently execute and invest through business cycles is a defining competitive advantage for ITW. The emphasis on customer-back innovation is critical for achieving high-quality organic growth, establishing trusted relationships with customers to address their most pressing needs. ITW operates in industries where it can generate significant, long-term competitive advantages, ensuring alignment with markets that have positive macro fundamentals and critical customer needs. The company’s focus on high-quality businesses supports its long-term growth potential, and it routinely assesses opportunities for selective acquisitions to reinforce this strategy. The 80/20 front-to-back practice excellence will continue to drive operational improvements across divisions, enhancing customer-facing performance and structural margin expansion. The company’s strategic management practices, including the evaluation of performance metrics and investment decisions, are designed to optimize resource allocation and enhance organizational capabilities. The firm’s ability to adapt to market dynamics and leverage its IT capabilities is fundamental to its ongoing transformation and performance excellence.
