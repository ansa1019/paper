Management believes that the financial statements for each of the years in the three-year period ended September 30, 2023, have been prepared in conformity with U.S. generally accepted accounting principles appropriate in the circumstances. The company's disclosure controls and procedures ensure that material information required to be disclosed is recorded, processed, summarized, and communicated to management and reported within the required time periods. In meeting its responsibility for the reliability of the financial statements, management relies on a system of internal accounting controls designed to provide reasonable assurance that assets are safeguarded and transactions are executed in accordance with management's authorization. The audit committee of the board of directors, composed solely of independent directors, is responsible for overseeing the company's financial reporting process. The company's management is responsible for establishing and maintaining adequate internal control over financial reporting. With the participation of the chief executive officer and the chief financial officer, management conducted an evaluation of the effectiveness of internal control over financial reporting based on the framework and criteria established in internal control - integrated framework (2013). The company maintains a conservative financial structure to provide the strength and flexibility necessary to achieve strategic objectives and efficiently deploy cash where needed worldwide to fund operations, complete acquisitions, and sustain long-term growth. Emerson's financial position is strong, with total assets of $43 billion and stockholders' equity of $21 billion, providing resources available for reinvestment in existing businesses, strategic acquisitions, and managing its capital structure. The company continues to generate substantial operating cash flow, including over $2.7 billion from continuing operations in 2023. The increase in operating cash flow reflects higher earnings and is indicative of the company's operational efficiency and capability to support its business strategies. Operating working capital as a percent of sales was 8.5 percent compared with 7.2 percent in 2022, reflecting higher inventory levels to support sales growth. The heritage AspenTech acquisition increased operating working capital, demonstrating the integration of technology capabilities into the operational framework. The company has taken significant actions to accelerate the transformation of its portfolio through strategic acquisitions and divestitures of non-core businesses, which are integral to creating a cohesive industrial technology portfolio. The acquisition of National Instruments Corporation, which provides software-connected automated test and measurement systems, is expected to enhance the company's technology resources and capabilities, enabling enterprises to bring products to market faster and at a lower cost. The company's investments in technology management and digital transformation are aimed at enhancing operational flexibility and supporting business innovation. The financial review indicates that management closely monitors operating profit and operating profit margin to evaluate past performance and actions required to improve profitability, which is a critical aspect of leveraging IT capabilities to achieve competitive advantage. The company's strategic resource allocation and investment decisions are designed to optimize performance outcomes and enhance organizational capabilities, ensuring alignment with business needs and market dynamics.
