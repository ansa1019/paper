Truist continues to activate its integrated relationship management approach, which is designed to deepen client relationships and bring the full breadth and depth of Truist's products and services to meet clients' financial needs. The company made critical progress on core bank conversions, including migrating heritage BB&T clients to the Truist ecosystem, and completed the wealth brokerage and trust transitions and the mortgage systems transition. Truist introduced the new Truist digital app for retail, wealth, and small business commercial clients, launching a digital commerce platform offering Truist-branded products in a goal-based, mobile-optimized experience. The company's strategy emphasizes driving innovation and remaining attuned to evolving client preferences to succeed in an intensely competitive environment. Truist's executive leadership changes reflect a commitment to executing on the strategy agreed upon in the merger, with a focus on achieving benefits from the merger, including anticipated synergies through cost-saving and the integrated relationship management approach. The company actively supports teammates, clients, and communities, demonstrating a commitment to investing in and serving all clients, regardless of their financial journey. Truist's management evaluates and adjusts the company's business strategy annually in the context of the current operating environment, considering the financial condition and performance of the company and expectations for future economic activity. The company recognizes the importance of mobilizing and deploying IT-based resources in combination with leveraging the value of other resources and capabilities to transform into a competitive force. Truist's technology risk framework provides the foundation for technology risk strategy, program, and oversight, defining key objectives, operating model components, risk domains, and capabilities to manage this risk. The company maintains a comprehensive risk management framework supported by people, processes, and systems to identify, measure, monitor, manage, and report significant risks arising from its exposures and business activities. Truist's cybersecurity risk program is overseen by executive leadership and the board, with regular updates on the status of the cybersecurity risk program, including information security risks and incidents. The company has invested substantial resources to manage and reduce cybersecurity risk, while recognizing that it is not possible to completely eliminate this risk. Truist's framework requires annual exercises to test preparedness against cyber threats, which are designed to assess the viability of crisis response and management programs. The company's risk management framework promotes the execution of business strategies and objectives in alignment with its risk appetite, ensuring that management considers how the interest rate risk position could be impacted by changes in balance sheet mix. Truist's commitment to operational flexibility and strategic resource allocation is evident in its approach to managing risks associated with integrating heritage BB&T and heritage SunTrust. The company employs strict underwriting criteria governing the degree of risk assumed and the diversity of the loan portfolio, ensuring that it meets the credit needs of its clients while pursuing a balanced strategy of loan profitability, loan growth, and loan quality.
