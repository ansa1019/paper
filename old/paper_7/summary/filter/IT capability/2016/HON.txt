In 2016, Honeywell successfully navigated a challenging macro-economic environment, executing on cost reduction activities, accelerating capital deployment strategies, and improving the growth portfolio through acquisitions and divestitures. The company maintained R&D spending at 5% of sales and introduced new products aligned with global macroeconomic trends in energy, safety, security, productivity, and global urbanization. Honeywell enhanced its software capabilities through the creation of a software center in the United States, staffing over 730 full-time product software engineers, in addition to more than 11,000 existing software engineers. The company deployed over $7.5 billion in capital, with over $2.5 billion allocated to mergers and acquisitions focused on software and technology, increasing alignment with macro trends such as energy efficiency and productivity. Honeywell's strategy to become a software-industrial company emphasizes products and services that facilitate connectivity across various sectors, including aviation and building technologies. The company aims to drive profitable organic growth through R&D and technological excellence, delivering innovative products that customers value. The successful integration of recently acquired businesses is critical to achieving operational flexibility and enhancing overall firm capability. Honeywell's focus on enhancing operational efficiency includes maintaining and improving the company's cost structure through manufacturing and administrative process improvements. The company also emphasizes the importance of effective working capital management to increase capital availability for strategic acquisitions and investments. The strategic alignment of business processes with technological capabilities is essential for supporting organizational goals and enhancing performance metrics. Honeywell's commitment to digital transformation and IT deployment reflects its recognition of the evolving nature of IT capabilities, which encompass the coordination of assets and internal processes, as well as interfirm transactions and relationships. The company's investment in technology infrastructure and governance practices aims to leverage IT resources effectively, ensuring that business needs are met with timely and reliable information management. The integration of technology capabilities into business strategies is viewed as a key driver of competitive advantage, enabling Honeywell to respond agilely to market dynamics and consumer demands. The company's approach to innovation partnerships and strategic resource allocation is designed to enhance its market positioning and operational effectiveness, ultimately contributing to improved financial performance and shareholder value.
