Paccar is a global technology company whose truck segment includes the design and manufacture of high-quality commercial trucks. The company has launched new vehicle technologies that provide customers real-time diagnostic information to enhance their vehicle operating performance. Kenworth TruckTech+ and Peterbilt SmartLINQ diagnostic systems are in production on new class 8 trucks equipped with the PACCAR MX-13 engine. Additionally, predictive cruise control integrates cruise control with global positioning system data to anticipate road contours, enabling the PACCAR MX-13 engine to achieve outstanding fuel economy. Kenworth and Peterbilt have developed additional technologies to enhance customers' driver performance and profitability, such as the Driver Performance Assistant, which provides real-time coaching on driving behavior. The company’s capital investments were $308.4 million in 2015, reflecting a focus on enhancing operational efficiency and supporting business needs. Research and development expenses were $239.8 million, aimed at new product development and enhanced aftermarket support. The company’s financial services segment derives earnings from financing or leasing PACCAR products, supporting a portfolio of loans and leases with total assets of $12.25 billion. The global breadth of PACCAR Financial Services and its rigorous credit application process contribute to its capability in managing financial activities effectively. The company’s investments in worldwide capital projects and R&D have significantly increased the operating capacity and efficiency of its facilities, enhancing the competitive advantage of its premium products. The company routinely monitors its financial exposure to global financial conditions and counterparties, ensuring effective resource allocation and risk management. The company’s strategic resource allocation includes funding for working capital, capital expenditures, and R&D, which are primarily sourced from cash provided by operations. The company has also established a systematic methodology for determining the allowance for credit losses related to its loans and finance leases, reflecting a strong capability in information management and operational flexibility. The company’s commitment to enhancing its technology infrastructure and capabilities is evident in its focus on digital transformation and the integration of advanced technologies into its operations. The company believes that its cash balances, collections on existing finance receivables, and access to capital markets will continue to provide sufficient resources to maintain liquidity and financial stability. The company’s strategic management practices are designed to prevent unreasonable risk and ensure compliance with environmental regulations, reflecting an understanding of the importance of governance contexts in its operations. Overall, PACCAR’s IT capabilities and investments in technology resources are integral to its business strategy, enabling the company to respond effectively to market dynamics and enhance its organizational performance.
