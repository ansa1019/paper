Duke Energy's management discussion and analysis emphasizes the importance of operational excellence and strategic initiatives that transform the energy future with a focus on customers, employees, operations, and growth. The company recognizes that its IT capabilities have evolved to encompass the coordination of its assets and internal processes, as well as interfirm transactions and relationships. This evolution underscores the necessity of mobilizing and deploying IT-based resources in conjunction with leveraging other resources and capabilities to support business strategies. The acquisition of Piedmont Natural Gas reflects the growing importance of natural gas infrastructure and establishes a platform for future growth, which is critical in enhancing operational flexibility and aligning with market dynamics. Duke Energy's commitment to modernization of the power grid aims to improve reliability and flexibility, supporting increased distributed energy sources. The company also focuses on stakeholder engagement to ensure that regulatory rules benefit all customers, which is essential for strategic resource allocation and market responsiveness. In 2015, Duke Energy advanced several strategic initiatives, including significant investments in renewable energy projects, which are integral to responding to customer interest in clean energy resources while increasing diversity in the generation portfolio. The company plans to invest between $4 billion and $5 billion annually in regulated utilities business growth projects, with many of these projects expected to be recovered through riders. Duke Energy's operational excellence is demonstrated through its commitment to safety and environmental performance, which is essential for maintaining competitive advantage. The company also emphasizes the importance of information management capabilities, which involve providing data and information to users with appropriate levels of accuracy, timeliness, and reliability. This capability is vital for decision-making speed and operational efficiency, ensuring that Duke Energy can respond effectively to changing business needs and directions. The ongoing evaluation of financial performance based on non-GAAP measures, including adjusted earnings and adjusted segment income, reflects the company's focus on aligning its operational metrics with strategic objectives. Duke Energy's governance practices ensure that IT resources are effectively integrated into its overall business strategy, enhancing organizational capabilities and performance outcomes. The company continues to assess its IT architecture and infrastructure capabilities to support its evolving business processes and customer relationships. As part of its strategic management practices, Duke Energy is committed to leveraging its IT systems to optimize performance and drive innovation, which is critical in a rapidly changing energy landscape. The company's focus on compliance with regulatory requirements and environmental standards further underscores the importance of integrating IT capabilities into its operational framework, enabling effective resource management and organizational change.
