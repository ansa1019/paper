Autodesk's vision is to help people imagine, design, and create a better world through the development of software and services for designers, architects, engineers, and digital artists. The company is undergoing a transition from traditional desktop software to cloud, social, and mobile computing, which represents a significant shift in its technology and business model. This transition is supported by the introduction of cloud-based offerings such as Autodesk BIM 360, PLM 360, SIM 360, and Fusion 360, which provide tools that enhance design, collaboration, and data management processes. The firm believes that customer adoption of these offerings will grow as industries leverage the scalable computing power and flexibility of these services. Autodesk's strategy focuses on leading customers to new cloud and mobile platforms, which entails both technological and business model shifts. The company has also introduced more flexible licensing options to accommodate project-based users and small businesses, aiming to increase its subscription base and customer value. The growth strategy includes enhancing product functionality and expanding offerings through internal development and acquisitions, which can accelerate the delivery of product capabilities to customers. Autodesk emphasizes the importance of mobilizing IT-based resources in conjunction with leveraging the value of other resources and capabilities to transform the firm into a competitive force. The company's technological leadership and extensive global network of distributors and resellers provide a competitive advantage, facilitating the rapid deployment and support of its products. The firm aims to address complex challenges such as globalization, urbanization, and sustainable design, which drive customers to seek improved performance and competitiveness. The breadth of Autodesk's technology and product line enables customers to tackle diverse problems across various industries, enhancing their operational efficiency and effectiveness. The company recognizes that its IT capabilities are critical in supporting business needs and driving innovation, as well as in managing transitions to new business models. Autodesk's commitment to improving its product functionality and expanding its offerings is integral to its strategic resource allocation and investment decisions, which are designed to enhance organizational capabilities and performance outcomes. The firm anticipates that its ongoing business model transition will lead to increased long-term revenue growth by expanding total subscriptions and customer value over time. The company is also focused on managing its operational costs while making essential investments to drive growth, ensuring that it remains agile and responsive to market dynamics and consumer demands.
