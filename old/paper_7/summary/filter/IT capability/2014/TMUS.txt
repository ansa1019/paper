The financial condition and results of operations discussed throughout management's discussion and analysis of financial condition and results of operations are those of T-Mobile US, Inc. and its consolidated subsidiaries. T-Mobile is the fastest growing wireless company in the U.S., providing wireless communications services to over 55 million customers. The un-carrier proposition seeks to listen to the customer, address their pain points, and improve the wireless experience for all. In 2014, T-Mobile continued to address customer pain points with the launch of phases of the un-carrier proposition, which included contract freedom, test drive, music freedom, wi-fi un-leashed, and data stash. The company's primary financial focus is on growing adjusted EBITDA, which increased to $5.6 billion in 2014, driven by increasing revenues from affordable wireless communication services. The ability to acquire and retain branded postpaid customers is crucial for revenue generation, with total branded postpaid net customer additions significantly improving in 2014. Improvements in customer retention were driven by the success of the un-carrier proposition and strong customer response to promotions. The company has focused on retaining customers through churn reduction initiatives to enhance customer experience. T-Mobile's liquidity sources include cash generated from operations and proceeds from the issuance of long-term debt and stock. The company has completed the process of upgrading its network to LTE, covering 265 million people as of December 31, 2014, and is targeting 300 million by the end of 2015. Cash capital expenditures for property and equipment, primarily related to network modernization, were $4.3 billion in 2014. T-Mobile expects to continue investing in its network infrastructure to enhance service delivery and operational efficiency. The company has entered into agreements for the acquisition of spectrum licenses, which are essential for expanding LTE coverage and enhancing its technology portfolio. The integration of technology capabilities is critical for T-Mobile to maintain competitive advantage and respond to market dynamics. The company's strategic resource allocation focuses on enhancing its digital infrastructure and operational capabilities to support business transformation and improve firm performance. The management's evaluation of performance metrics includes adjusted EBITDA as a benchmark to assess operating performance compared to competitors. T-Mobile's strategic management practices emphasize the importance of leveraging IT capabilities to support business needs and enhance customer relationships. The company recognizes the significance of information management capability, which includes providing accurate, timely, and reliable data to users while adapting to changing business requirements.
