Citi has made substantial progress on its transformation, including continued investments in technology, risk, controls, data, and finance programs, with approximately 25% of these program investments related to technology. The firm’s ongoing transformation efforts aim to strengthen risk and controls while improving value to customers, clients, and shareholders. Citi's CEO has prioritized enhancing the risk and control environment, which includes establishing a chief administrative officer organization to centralize program management. The Institutional Clients Group (ICG) has continued to invest in its transformation, which is reflected in the increase of operating expenses primarily driven by business-led investments and volume-related expenses. The firm’s revenues increased 5% in 2022, driven by higher net interest income, which was partially offset by lower non-interest revenues. The strong performance in Treasury and Trade Solutions (TTS) was attributed to higher interest rates and business actions, including balance sheet optimization and managing deposit pricing, which deepened relationships with existing clients and increased new clients across segments. ICG's management of the markets businesses involves daily monitoring and evaluation of market factors, including interest rates and client activity levels. The firm has also focused on enhancing its technology infrastructure to support business processes and improve operational efficiency. The ongoing investments in technology and transformation are expected to continue impacting Citi's financial performance positively, as the firm adapts to changing market dynamics and consumer demands. The firm’s strategic resource allocation reflects a commitment to leveraging technology capabilities to enhance organizational effectiveness and drive performance gains. The focus on digital transformation and IT deployment is integral to Citi's strategy to maintain competitive advantage and respond to evolving business needs. Additionally, the firm has emphasized the importance of information management capability, which includes providing data and information to users with appropriate levels of accuracy, timeliness, reliability, security, and access. These efforts are crucial for mobilizing and deploying IT-based resources in combination with other capabilities to further organizational goals and enhance business value. The transformation initiatives also include addressing regulatory compliance and improving governance contexts, which are essential for navigating the complexities of the current business environment. Overall, Citi's commitment to investing in technology and enhancing its IT capabilities is a key component of its strategy to improve operational flexibility, market responsiveness, and overall firm performance.
