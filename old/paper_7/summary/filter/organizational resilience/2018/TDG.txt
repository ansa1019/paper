Improving our cost structure is essential for maintaining and continuously enhancing our lean cost structure through detailed attention to the cost of each of the products that we offer and our organizational structure, with a focus on reducing the cost of each. The company made an organizational realignment of certain businesses comprising the power & control, airframe, and the non-aviation segments, effective October 1, 2017. This organizational change reflects our commitment to adaptation and operational flexibility in response to internal and external pressures. The operating results for the year ended September 30, 2017, were reclassified to conform to the presentation for the fiscal year ended September 30, 2018, indicating a systematic approach to maintaining organizational coherence and effectiveness. This realignment and reclassification process demonstrates our resilience strategies in navigating complex situations and ensuring business continuity. By focusing on resource allocation and operational management, we aim to enhance our organizational capabilities and performance under pressure, thereby fostering an environment conducive to recovery and transformation amidst adversity. The emphasis on improving our cost structure aligns with our broader objectives of sustaining organizational health and ensuring that we can confront unexpected situations effectively. These strategic initiatives are designed to bolster our competitive advantage and support our long-term goals of survival and growth in a turbulent market environment. Through this approach, we are not only addressing immediate challenges but also positioning ourselves for future success by enhancing our organizational agility and responsiveness to disruptive changes.
