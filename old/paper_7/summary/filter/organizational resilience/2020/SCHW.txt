Throughout the extraordinary macroeconomic environment that persisted during 2020, Schwab continued to execute on key strategic initiatives, advancing the company's strategic goals to drive scale, monetization, and segmentation in ways that benefit clients. The impact of COVID-19, along with social and political turmoil, created an unprecedented combination of personal and macroeconomic challenges for clients, employees, and stockholders. Schwab maintained its disciplined approach to capital management, helping sustain significant balance sheet growth. The company's ability to support client activity levels and develop trusted relationships was crucial in navigating these challenges. Schwab's operations remained consistent with its enterprise risk management framework, aligning risk exposures with the company's risk appetite. The integration of TD Ameritrade's operations is expected to occur over 18 to 36 months, and the company anticipates incurring significant acquisition and integration-related costs throughout this process. Schwab's risk management process includes risk identification and assessment, risk measurement, risk monitoring and reporting, and risk mitigation controls, which are vital for maintaining operational continuity and resilience. The company has established a backup and recovery infrastructure, including facilities for backup and communications, a geographically dispersed workforce, and routine testing of business continuity and disaster recovery plans. Schwab's comprehensive risk management program is designed to identify and manage risks and their potential financial and reputational impacts. The company's operational risk management is focused on maintaining the integrity and resilience of critical business functions and technology systems. Schwab's ability to adapt and respond effectively to environmental disruptions is supported by its strategic initiatives and operational flexibility. The integration of acquisitions, such as TD Ameritrade and USAA-IMCO, is expected to enhance Schwab's capabilities and contribute to its resilience strategies. The company has also implemented measures to monitor and manage liquidity risk, ensuring ongoing and sufficient liquidity to meet operational needs and satisfy regulatory requirements under both normal and stressed conditions. Schwab's capital management strategy includes maintaining capital levels sufficient to support anticipated balance sheet growth and providing financial support to subsidiaries. The company's focus on driving efficiency while managing spending in a disciplined manner has helped maintain a ratio of expenses to client assets. Schwab's commitment to organizational resilience is reflected in its proactive measures to address potential risks and uncertainties, ensuring the capacity to continue operations during incidents regardless of duration. The company continuously monitors systems and collaborates with government agencies and other financial institutions to address potential threats, thereby reinforcing its operational resilience and adaptability in a turbulent business environment.
