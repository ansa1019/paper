The company has established a comprehensive risk management program to identify and manage various risks, including operational, credit, market, liquidity, and compliance risks, which are essential for maintaining organizational resilience. This program incorporates risk identification and assessment, risk measurement, risk monitoring and reporting, and risk mitigation, reflecting a systemic perspective on organizational health. Senior management actively participates in the risk management process, developing policies and procedures that empower specific business and control units to manage risks effectively. The global risk committee oversees these efforts, identifying emerging risks and assessing risk management practices, thereby reinforcing business accountability for risk management. The company has implemented a culture of risk awareness, supported by an enterprise risk management framework that aligns with its corporate strategy, ensuring that risk appetite is defined and approved by the board of directors.

Operational risks arise from inadequate or failed internal processes, people, and systems, as well as external events. The company relies on a robust system of internal controls and risk management practices to keep operational risks within acceptable limits. To minimize business interruptions, the company maintains a backup and recovery infrastructure, including routine testing of business continuity and disaster recovery plans. Information security risks are addressed through a comprehensive program designed to protect systems, client accounts, and data, with continuous monitoring to identify and respond to potential threats.

The company emphasizes the importance of adaptability and operational flexibility in response to environmental perturbations and unexpected situations. It has established liquidity policies to ensure ongoing and sufficient liquidity to meet operational needs, even during stress conditions. Regular liquidity stress testing is conducted to develop a consolidated view of liquidity risk exposures, ensuring the company's ability to maintain sufficient liquidity during market-related or company-specific liquidity stress events.

The company’s capital management strategy aims to support its business strategy while maintaining regulatory capital requirements. It conducts regular capital stress testing to assess the potential financial impacts of adverse macroeconomic and company-specific events, thereby ensuring the organization can absorb unanticipated losses. The capital contingency plan provides guidelines for responding to low probability/high impact capital events, reinforcing the company’s resilience in challenging conditions.

The organization’s ability to attract and retain clients, develop trusted relationships, and grow client assets is critical to its competitive advantage. The company has positioned itself to benefit from increasing interest rates while limiting exposure to falling rates, demonstrating strategic response capabilities in a volatile business environment. The management believes that strong client momentum and innovative service offerings contribute to the company’s resilience and operational continuity, enabling it to thrive despite external pressures and market uncertainties.
