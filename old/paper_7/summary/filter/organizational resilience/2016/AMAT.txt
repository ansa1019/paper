Applied's strategic priorities include developing products that help solve customers' challenges at technology inflections, expanding its served market opportunities in the semiconductor and display industries, and growing its services business. The company recognizes that its future operating results depend to a considerable extent on its ability to maintain a competitive advantage in the equipment and service products it provides. Applied's investments in research, development, and engineering are critical to ensuring the availability of innovative technology that meets the current and projected requirements of its customers' most advanced designs. The mobility trend remains the largest influence on industry spending, driving semiconductor device manufacturers to continually improve their ability to deliver high-performance, low-power processors and affordable memories. Applied's results can vary significantly year-over-year and quarter-over-quarter due to variable industry conditions, as demand for manufacturing equipment and services can change depending on supply and demand for chips, display technologies, and other electronic devices, as well as global economic and market conditions. The company has maintained its commitment to investing in research and development to continue to offer new products and technologies, which is essential for adapting to changing market demands and maintaining operational flexibility. 

Applied's operational management includes a focus on optimizing equipment and fab performance and productivity through integrated solutions. The company fulfills customer demand through a global distribution system with trained service engineers located in close proximity to customer sites, which enhances its ability to respond effectively to environmental disruptions. The semiconductor systems segment's financial results reflect steady overall demand for semiconductor equipment, with continued investments by memory customers in technology upgrades and additional capacity. New orders for fiscal 2016 increased across all segments primarily due to higher demand for display and semiconductor equipment, indicating the company's capacity to adapt to market changes and customer needs.

Applied's backlog consists of orders for which written authorizations have been accepted and assigned shipment dates within the next 12 months, which is indicative of the company's ability to manage operational continuity and respond to customer demands. The company has also recognized the importance of maintaining a strong liquidity position to address potential resource constraints and ensure business continuity. Applied's management believes that cash generated from operations, together with existing cash balances and borrowing capability, will be sufficient to satisfy its liquidity requirements for the next 12 months. 

The company's ability to absorb strain and preserve functioning despite adversity is reflected in its strategic initiatives and operational practices, which are designed to enhance organizational resilience. Applied's focus on maintaining a competitive edge through innovation and strategic adaptation positions it to confront and benefit from sudden disruptive changes in the market. The emphasis on collaboration networks and inter-organizational collaboration further supports the company's resilience strategies, enabling it to navigate complex situations and environmental perturbations effectively.
