The company designs, manufactures, and markets mobile communication and media devices, personal computers, and portable digital music players, and sells a variety of related software, services, accessories, networking solutions, and third-party digital content and applications. The company believes that focused investments in research and development are critical to its future growth and competitive position in the marketplace and are directly related to timely development of new and enhanced products that are central to the company's core business strategy. The company continues to evaluate the realizability of deferred tax assets quarterly by assessing the need for and the amount of a valuation allowance. The company anticipates utilizing approximately $13.0 billion for capital expenditures during 2015, including significant investments in product tooling and manufacturing process equipment, data centers, and corporate facilities and infrastructure. The company also expects to open about 25 new retail stores, with approximately three-quarters located outside of the U.S. The company has entered into interest rate swaps to manage interest rate risk on its long-term debt, allowing it to effectively convert fixed-rate payments into floating-rate payments or vice versa. The company recognizes that its existing balances of cash, cash equivalents, and marketable securities will be sufficient to satisfy its working capital needs, capital asset purchases, outstanding commitments, and other liquidity requirements associated with its existing operations over the next 12 months. The company has not entered into any transactions with unconsolidated entities that expose it to material continuing risks or contingent liabilities. The company manages its business primarily on a geographic basis, which reflects its ability to respond effectively to environmental disruptions and transform itself to emerge stronger from challenging situations. The company’s operating segments provide similar hardware and software products and similar services, which enhances its operational flexibility and adaptability in the face of external pressures and market volatility. The company’s growth in net sales during 2014 was driven by increased demand for its products, particularly the successful introduction of new iPhone models and the expansion of its retail segment. The company’s strategic initiatives include a significant increase in its share repurchase program, which reflects its commitment to returning capital to shareholders while maintaining operational continuity. The company’s ability to absorb strain and preserve functioning despite adversity is evidenced by its strong performance across various operating segments, even in the face of declining sales in certain product categories. The company’s strategic response to competitive pressures has included product pricing actions and investments in marketing and advertising, which are essential for maintaining its competitive advantage. The company’s organizational practices emphasize the importance of collaboration networks and inter-organizational collaboration, which are vital for enhancing organizational resilience and ensuring long-term sustainability. The company’s proactive measures in crisis management and risk assessment are designed to mitigate potential disruptions and ensure recovery from unexpected situations. The company’s focus on innovation and adaptability is reflected in its ongoing commitment to enhancing its product offerings and expanding its market presence, which positions it favorably in a rapidly changing business environment.
