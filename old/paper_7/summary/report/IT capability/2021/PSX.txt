Phillips 66 is committed to operating excellence, which guides its approach to protecting health and safety, as well as environmental stewardship, reliability, and cost efficiency. The company employs rigorous training and audit programs to drive ongoing improvement in both personal and process safety, striving for zero incidents. Continuous improvement in operational efficiency is a fundamental requirement. The company’s strategic initiatives include a disciplined capital allocation process to invest in projects expected to generate competitive returns, particularly in the midstream and chemicals segments, as well as renewable fuels projects aimed at advancing a lower-carbon future. In 2021, Phillips 66 progressed on strategic initiatives to position itself for a lower-carbon future, including the establishment of greenhouse gas (GHG) emission intensity reduction targets. The company expects to reduce GHG emission intensity by 30% for scope 1 and 2 emissions and by 15% for scope 3 emissions by 2030, below 2019 levels. The acquisition of a 16% interest in Novonix Limited, a company that develops technology for lithium-ion batteries, reflects an actionable investment in technology resources. The company’s capital budget for 2022 includes significant allocations for digital transformation projects, indicating a focus on enhancing its information technology capabilities. The budget also emphasizes growth capital supporting lower-carbon opportunities, which aligns with the evolving scope of IT capabilities that encompass the coordination of a firm’s assets and internal processes. Phillips 66's operational flexibility is enhanced through investments in technology infrastructure and process automation, which are critical for optimizing utilization rates and product yield at its refineries. The company’s focus on strategic resource allocation is evident in its plans to enhance refining returns by increasing throughput of advantaged feedstocks and improving yields. The marketing and specialties segment is expected to continue developing and enhancing its retail network, including energy transition opportunities, which reflects an understanding of market dynamics and consumer choice. The company actively monitors its capital allocation and performance metrics to ensure alignment with organizational goals and market responsiveness. Phillips 66’s commitment to fostering an inclusive workplace that enables its diverse workforce to innovate and create value is integral to its high-performing organization strategy. The focus on attracting, developing, and retaining individuals with the necessary knowledge and skills supports the implementation of its business strategy and enhances overall firm capability. The company’s operational efficiency and performance improvement initiatives are supported by robust information management capabilities that ensure data accuracy, reliability, and timely access to information, which are essential for effective decision-making and strategic alignment.
