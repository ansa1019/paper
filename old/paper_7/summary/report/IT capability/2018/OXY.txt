Occidental's strategy focuses on delivering a unique shareholder value proposition through continual enhancements to its asset quality, organizational capability, and innovative technical applications that provide competitive advantages. The oil and gas segment benefits from scale, technical expertise, environmental and safety leadership, and commercial and governmental collaboration, allowing Occidental to bring additional production quickly to market and extend the life of older fields at lower costs. The company emphasizes the importance of mobilizing and deploying IT-based resources in combination with leveraging the value of other resources and capabilities to transform the firm into a competitive force. Occidental's operations are characterized by a disciplined approach to capital expenditures with a focus on returns and an emphasis on creating value, further enhancing existing positions through improved operational efficiencies and innovative ideas. The midstream and marketing segment strives to maximize realized value by optimizing the use of its committed pipeline and export capacities and by providing access to domestic and international markets. Additionally, the segment evaluates opportunities across the value chain to generate returns, utilizing its assets to provide services to Occidental subsidiaries and third parties. Occidental's capital is employed to sustain or expand assets to improve competitiveness and drive cost efficiencies. The company seeks to capitalize on its enhanced oil recovery leadership by developing carbon capture, utilization, and storage projects that promote innovative technologies and drive cost efficiencies while reducing emissions. Occidental's investment decisions are guided by a commitment to allocate free cash flow toward investments that generate the highest returns, alongside returning cash to shareholders through dividends and share repurchases. The firm’s IT capabilities are integral to supporting business needs, enhancing operational efficiency, and facilitating strategic resource allocation. Occidental conducts its operations with a focus on sustainability, health, safety, and environmental and social responsibility, which are critical in managing risks associated with commodity price volatility and operational challenges. The company’s ability to add reserves depends on the success of improved recovery, extension, and discovery projects, which rely on technology improvements and market conditions. Occidental's management practices include a detailed review of proved undeveloped reserves to ensure timely conversion to proved developed reserves, supported by a five-year development plan. The firm’s operational flexibility and market responsiveness are enhanced by its strategic alignment of technology resources and capabilities, which are essential for maintaining competitive positioning and achieving performance excellence. Overall, Occidental’s integrated business model leverages its technological and operational capabilities to navigate market dynamics and drive long-term value creation.
