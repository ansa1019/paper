The implementation of certified EHR technology is expected to incur both capital costs and operating expenses, which are projected to continue over all stages of implementation of meaningful use. The company’s ability to leverage its scale and market positions to enhance profitability is seen as a significant opportunity, including the expansion of its support infrastructure for clinical and support functions such as physician credentialing and electronic medical recordkeeping. The organization is focused on selectively developing and acquiring new hospitals and outpatient facilities, believing that challenges faced by the hospital industry may spur consolidation. The company’s strategic management practices include leveraging key components of its support infrastructure, such as revenue cycle management and health care group purchasing, to enhance operational efficiency. The company’s centralized management of business processes is expected to enable it to continue managing costs effectively. The organization recognizes that the emergence of digital technologies and the need for operational flexibility are crucial for maintaining competitive advantage in a highly competitive health care business environment.
