BB&T's business has become more dynamic and complex in recent years, leading management to annually evaluate and adjust the company's business strategy in the context of the current operating environment. New technologies and evolving consumer preferences will put pressure on market share and customer loyalty. The company faces challenges associated with regulatory reform and initiatives and IT projects, which are critical for maintaining competitive advantage. The integration of acquired entities' information technology systems is a significant aspect of merger-related and restructuring charges. The management of IT resources is essential for supporting business needs and enhancing operational efficiency. BB&T emphasizes the importance of mobilizing and deploying IT-based resources in combination with leveraging the value of other resources and capabilities. The ability to provide data and information to users with appropriate levels of accuracy, timeliness, reliability, security, confidentiality, connectivity, and access is crucial for responding to changing business needs and directions. The company has established a risk management framework that includes oversight of technology-related risks, with the Chief Risk Officer responsible for communicating significant risks to executive management and the board of directors. BB&T's risk culture promotes predictable and consistent performance, ensuring that risks are managed effectively. The organization focuses on building strong, profitable client relationships, which involves assessing their financial position and background, thus integrating technology management into its overall strategy. The company's approach to credit risk involves careful initial underwriting and ongoing monitoring of individual loans and lending relationships, which is supported by technology. BB&T's asset/liability management process is designed to achieve stable net interest income and assure liquidity by coordinating the volumes, maturities, or repricing opportunities of earning assets, deposits, and borrowed funds. The management of market risk includes evaluating the impact of changes in interest rates on net interest income and capital, with a focus on strategic pricing of asset and liability accounts. BB&T utilizes various derivative instruments to manage economic risks related to its portfolios. The company maintains a liquid asset buffer and follows regulation requirements to ensure adequate liquidity. BB&T's management of liquidity risk involves monitoring funding concentrations, diversification, and maturity distribution, with a strong focus on its reputation to ensure continued access to client deposits. The company also evaluates the impact of becoming subject to full liquidity coverage ratio requirements, ensuring its capital position is adequate to absorb losses and maintain a highly capitalized status. The active management of subsidiaries' equity capital is crucial for maintaining confidence among depositors and other funding sources. BB&T's commitment to technology integration and management is evident in its strategic objectives, which aim to enhance organizational capabilities and performance outcomes in a competitive environment.
