AT&T's business strategies reflect integrated product offerings that cut across product lines and utilize shared assets. The company plans to leverage its strong fiber and wireless assets, broad distribution, and integrated product offers to strengthen its overall market position. As part of its transformation activities, AT&T expects operations and support expense improvements through 2024, driven by efficiencies in network operations through automation and digitization. The company is focused on enhancing operational efficiency and effectiveness, which includes restructuring businesses, sunsetting legacy networks, and improving customer service through digital transformation. The deployment of open radio access network (open ran) is expected to foster lower network costs and improved operational efficiencies, allowing for continued investment in broadband networks. AT&T anticipates that the software benefits of its 5G wireless technology will result in a more efficient use of capital and lower network-related expenses in the coming years. The company is also pursuing a simplified go-to-market strategy for 5G in underpenetrated markets, which is expected to contribute to wireless subscriber and service revenue growth. The integration of wireless and fiber services is a key focus, as AT&T aims to provide broadband offerings that allow customers to integrate their home or business fixed services with mobile service. The company is committed to deploying fiber and enhancing its fiber footprint, which is expected to drive greater demand for broadband services. AT&T's operational flexibility is further enhanced by its strategic resource allocation, which includes investments in technology infrastructure and capabilities to support business needs. The company is also focused on improving its information management capability, ensuring data and information are provided to users with appropriate levels of accuracy, timeliness, reliability, and security. The ongoing capital investment for strategic initiatives, such as fiber and network upgrades, is expected to increase depreciation expense, reflecting the company's commitment to enhancing its technology portfolio. AT&T's operational performance is linked to its ability to manage subscriber churn effectively, which is critical for maximizing revenue growth and maintaining margins. The company's business wireline segment continues to reflect lower demand for legacy services, while growth in connectivity services is anticipated. AT&T's approach to managing its operations includes a focus on efficiency and effectiveness, with expectations for cost savings through AI-driven efficiencies in network design and customer support services. The company recognizes the importance of mobilizing and deploying IT-based resources in combination with leveraging the value of other resources and capabilities to transform into a competitive force. The strategic decisions made regarding technology deployment and resource management are expected to enhance AT&T's market agility and responsiveness to external influences. The company is also committed to fostering innovation partnerships and aligning its business strategy with evolving market dynamics to create value and improve performance outcomes.
