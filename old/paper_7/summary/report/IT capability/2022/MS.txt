The firm actively develops appropriate transition plans for its U.S. dollar LIBOR-linked contracts, overseen by a global steering committee with senior management oversight, to facilitate the transition to alternative reference rates. Effective risk management is vital to the success of business activities, supported by an enterprise risk management (ERM) framework that integrates diverse roles of risk management into a holistic structure. This framework facilitates the incorporation of risk assessment into decision-making processes across the firm, with policies and procedures in place to identify, measure, monitor, escalate, mitigate, and control principal risks, including operational, compliance, cybersecurity, and strategic risks. The firm's risk governance structure requires independent oversight, accountability of business divisions, and effective communication of risk matters. The Operations and Technology Committee of the Board oversees operations and technology strategy, including significant investments in support of such strategy, and monitors operational risk, including information security and cybersecurity risks. The firm’s risk appetite defines the aggregate level and types of risk that the firm is willing to accept to achieve its business objectives, which is linked to its short-term and long-term strategic, capital, and financial plans. The firm employs a comprehensive suite of risk limits and quantitative metrics to support its risk appetite statement, ensuring that risk-taking activities align with established limits. The risk management process incorporates stress testing and scenario analyses to assess the impact of market factors on exposures and capital position. The firm’s operational flexibility is enhanced by its ability to leverage technology resources and capabilities, which are essential in transforming the firm into a competitive force. The integration of technology into business processes supports the firm’s objectives and enhances its capability to respond to market dynamics. The firm’s commitment to continuous review and enhancement of methodologies and assumptions captures evolving risks associated with changes in market structure and dynamics, ensuring that strategic resource allocation aligns with business needs and market-based performance. The firm’s technology infrastructure supports the management of information processing capacity and enhances operational efficiency, thereby contributing to overall organizational effectiveness and performance improvement. The firm actively monitors and manages credit risk through established practices, ensuring that credit exposure is evaluated and controlled at the transaction, obligor, and portfolio levels. The evaluation of counterparties incorporates various risk factors, including financial strength and market position, which are critical in maintaining the integrity of the firm’s operations and supporting its strategic objectives. The firm’s ability to adapt to changing business environments and regulatory contexts is facilitated by its robust risk management framework and technology capabilities, which are integral to achieving its organizational goals and sustaining competitive advantage.
