Moody's is a provider of credit ratings, assessment services, credit, capital markets and economic research, data and analytical tools, and software solutions that support financial risk management activities. The company evaluates its reporting units on an annual basis, or more frequently if there are changes in the reporting structure due to acquisitions or realignments. Moody's allocates newly acquired goodwill to reporting units based on the reporting unit expected to benefit from the acquisition. The company has engaged in restructuring actions that require management to utilize significant estimates related to expenses for severance and other employee benefit costs, contract termination costs, and asset impairments. The company regularly assesses the likely outcomes of tax audits to determine the appropriateness of liabilities for uncertain tax positions. The preparation of financial statements requires Moody's to make estimates and judgments that affect reported amounts of assets and liabilities and related disclosures of contingent assets and liabilities. The company has updated its mortality assumption to reflect the latest information regarding future mortality expectations. Moody's believes that the assumptions used in its calculations are reasonable, but differences in actual experience or changes in assumptions could have a significant effect on the expenses, assets, and liabilities related to the company's retirement plans. The company evaluates its accounts receivable allowance by reviewing historical collection and adjustment experience and the current status of customer accounts, considering the economic environment of the customers. The company has provided deferred taxes for entities whose earnings are not considered indefinitely reinvested outside of the U.S. The company regularly evaluates its capital allocation strategy, including its global cash position and plans for repatriating or reinvesting foreign earnings. Moody's has engaged in acquisitions that impact the company's year-over-year comparative results, including the acquisition of Bureau van Dijk, which enhances the company's technology infrastructure to enable automation, innovation, and efficiency. The company has also made significant investments in technology to support its initiatives. The increase in operating and SG&A expenses reflects growth in both compensation and non-compensation costs, with notable drivers being higher costs to support the company's initiative to enhance technology infrastructure. The company’s revenue growth reflects strong demand for its credit research and ratings data feeds product lines, as well as ongoing demand for SaaS-based solutions. The company’s strategic decisions regarding investments in technology capabilities and infrastructure are essential for maintaining competitive advantage and operational efficiency. The company’s ability to leverage its IT capabilities effectively supports its business needs and enhances its market-based performance. The company recognizes that the outcomes of various matters, including legal proceedings and regulatory investigations, may impact its competitive positioning and operational capabilities. The company is subject to income taxes in various jurisdictions and regularly assesses the likely outcomes of tax audits.
