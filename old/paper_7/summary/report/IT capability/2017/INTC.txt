2017 was another record year for Intel, reflecting progress in shifting from a PC-centric to a data-centric company, driven by strategic investments in data-rich markets such as memory and autonomous driving. The firm achieved record revenue of $62.8 billion, with strong performance across data-centric businesses, which collectively grew 16% year over year. The company implemented changes to the Intel Inside program to enhance efficiency and effectiveness, impacting revenue classification and customer flexibility. Intel's growth strategy focuses on enhancing platforms and adjacent technologies to meet evolving consumer expectations and demands, particularly in smart homes and connected devices. The data center market is anticipated to surpass $70 billion by 2022, and Intel sees significant opportunities in cloud, networking, and analytics, driving higher growth through expanded product offerings. The company emphasizes the importance of mobilizing and deploying IT-based resources in combination with leveraging other capabilities to transform into a competitive force. Investments in research and development totaled $13.1 billion, reflecting a commitment to innovation and operational flexibility, particularly in data-centric areas. The company is focused on improving operational efficiency and resource allocation to enhance market responsiveness and competitive positioning. The transition to a data-centric model necessitates a robust technology infrastructure and effective IT governance to support business processes and enhance firm capabilities. Intel's strategic management practices are designed to align technology capabilities with business strategies, ensuring that IT investments contribute to overall firm performance and market-based performance metrics. The company recognizes the need for ongoing innovation partnerships and strategic resource allocation to maintain a competitive advantage in a rapidly evolving market landscape. The integration of advanced technologies and data analytics into business processes is critical for optimizing performance and achieving operational excellence. As the firm navigates market dynamics and external influences, it remains committed to leveraging its IT capabilities to enhance customer relationships and drive value creation across its operations. The focus on digital transformation and the deployment of IT resources is integral to Intel's strategy for achieving long-term growth and sustainability in the technology sector.
