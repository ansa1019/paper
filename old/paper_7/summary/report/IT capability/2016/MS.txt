Morgan Stanley, a financial holding company, is a global financial services firm that maintains significant market positions in each of its business segments. The results of operations may continue to be materially affected by competition, risk factors, and legislative, legal, and regulatory developments, which may have an adverse impact on the ability to achieve strategic objectives. The firm’s IT capabilities represent the application of physical or intangible IT resources such as technology, knowledge, practices, relationships, management skills, and business process understanding to further organizational goals. The discussion of results may contain forward-looking statements subject to risks and uncertainties that may cause actual results to differ materially. The firm focuses on information management capability, which is the ability to provide data and information to users with appropriate levels of accuracy, timeliness, reliability, security, confidentiality, connectivity, and access, and to tailor these in response to changing business needs and directions. IT capability has become key in transforming the firm into a competitive force. The firm’s IT capabilities have been evolving in scope to encompass the coordination of assets and internal processes and their interfirm transactions and relationships. The firm aims to improve return to shareholders and has established a target return on average common equity of 9% to 11%, excluding certain factors, to be achieved by 2017. This target is subject to successful execution of strategic objectives, which include revenue and profitability growth, expense efficiency, and sufficient capital. The firm’s liquidity risk management framework is designed to ensure access to adequate funding across a wide range of market conditions and time horizons, supporting the execution of business strategies. The required liquidity framework reflects the amount of liquidity to hold in both normal and stressed environments. The firm’s capital management strategy actively manages its consolidated capital position based on business opportunities, risks, capital availability, and rates of return, ensuring that total capital is at least equal to the sum of required equity for operating subsidiaries. The firm’s regulatory capital framework is based on Basel III standards, requiring maintenance of minimum risk-based and leverage capital ratios. The firm is also subject to a risk-based capital surcharge as a global systemically important bank. The capital plan requires submission to the Federal Reserve for approval before making capital distributions, including dividends and stock repurchases. The firm conducts annual resolution planning to describe its strategy for a rapid and orderly resolution under the U.S. bankruptcy code in the event of financial distress. The firm’s operational and financial activities are closely monitored, with significant judgment required in estimating provisions for income taxes, legal and regulatory contingencies, and other financial obligations. The firm’s IT governance and management practices support its operational efficiency and effectiveness, enabling it to respond to market dynamics and maintain competitive positioning.
