The global business services (GBS) organization is responsible for providing technology, processes, and standard data tools to enable the global business units (GBUs) and selling and market operations (SMOs) to better understand the business and better serve consumers and customers. GBS aims to provide world-class solutions at a low cost and with minimal capital investment. The company has undertaken an effort to focus and strengthen its business portfolio to compete in categories and with brands that are structurally attractive and that play to Procter & Gamble's strengths. The ongoing portfolio of businesses consists of 10 product categories where the company has leading market positions, strong brands, and consumer-meaningful product technologies. The company believes these strategies are right for the long-term health of the company and its objective of delivering total shareholder return in the top one-third of its peer group. The company is in the midst of a productivity and cost savings plan to reduce costs in the areas of supply chain, marketing, and overhead expenses, designed to accelerate cost reductions by streamlining management decision-making, manufacturing, and other work processes to fund the company's growth strategy. This plan includes significant investments in technology and innovation, which are critical to delivering balanced top-line growth, bottom-line growth, and value creation objectives. The company has also initiated certain non-manufacturing overhead reduction projects along with manufacturing and other supply chain cost improvement projects. The management's discussion and analysis (MD&A) provides an understanding of Procter & Gamble's financial condition, results of operations, and cash flows by focusing on changes in certain key measures from year to year. The company evaluates performance using various measures, including organic sales growth, core earnings per share, adjusted free cash flow, and adjusted free cash flow productivity, which provide investors with additional information about underlying results and trends. The company’s strategic focus aspires to serve the world's consumers better than its best competitors in every category and in every country in which it competes, delivering total shareholder return in the top one-third of its peer group. The company believes that innovation, driven by consumer insights and technology advancements, is essential for maintaining competitive advantage and market leadership. The GBS organization plays a vital role in enabling the GBUs and SMOs to leverage technology resources effectively, ensuring operational efficiency and enhancing customer relationships. The company’s approach to managing its technology portfolio includes a focus on productivity improvement across all elements of cost, including cost of goods sold, marketing and promotional expenses, and non-manufacturing overhead. The company also emphasizes the importance of mobilizing and deploying IT-based resources in combination with leveraging the value of other resources and capabilities to transform the firm into a competitive force. This transformation is supported by a commitment to improving operational effectiveness and organizational culture through enhanced clarity of roles and responsibilities, accountability, and incentive compensation programs.
