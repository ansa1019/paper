The Goldman Sachs Group, Inc. emphasizes the importance of mobilizing and deploying IT-based resources in combination with leveraging the value of other resources and capabilities to transform the firm into a competitive force. The firm’s IT capabilities represent the application of physical or intangible IT resources such as technology, knowledge, practices, relationships, management skills, and business process understanding to further organizational goals. Effective risk management is critical to success, and the firm maintains a comprehensive risk management framework that includes technology risk management, which reviews matters related to the design, development, deployment, and use of technology, including cyber security matters. The firm’s operational risk management framework is designed to minimize operational risks through extensive policies and procedures, training, and active participation of senior management in identifying and mitigating key operational risks. The firm employs various risk measures to monitor market risk, including value-at-risk (VaR) and stress testing, which are essential for assessing the impact of market dynamics on the firm’s performance. The firm utilizes technology to monitor market risk, including independent calculations of VaR and stress measures, and the ability to report risk measures across various dimensions, enhancing decision-making capabilities. The firm’s liquidity risk management strategy includes holding sufficient excess liquidity in the form of global core liquid assets (GCLA) to cover outflows during stressed periods, which is critical for maintaining operational flexibility. The GCLA is composed of unencumbered, highly liquid securities and cash, enabling the firm to meet immediate obligations without needing to sell other assets. The firm’s credit risk management process incorporates a robust framework for assessing, monitoring, and managing credit exposures, utilizing credit limits at various levels and employing risk mitigants such as collateral and hedging. The firm’s stress tests are designed to estimate the impact of severe market events on credit and market exposures, ensuring preparedness for potential liquidity crises. The firm’s governance structure includes a series of committees responsible for overseeing risk management practices, including the firmwide risk committee and the firmwide model risk control committee, which ensure that models used for risk assessment are validated and appropriate for intended use. The firm’s commitment to technology investment supports its ability to manage risks effectively, with systems in place to facilitate the collection of data used to analyze and assess operational risk exposure. The firm’s strategic resource allocation and investment decisions are guided by its comprehensive risk management framework, which integrates insights from various risk categories to enhance overall organizational effectiveness and performance.
