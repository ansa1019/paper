Johnson & Johnson and its subsidiaries have approximately 127,100 employees engaged in the research and development, manufacture, and sale of a broad range of products in the health care field, which requires substantial investments in continuing research to maintain competitive advantage. The company manages within a strategic framework with its credo as the foundation, believing that strategic operating principles such as being broadly based in human health care and managing the business for the long term are crucial for effectively responding to environmental disruptions. Management is focused on long-term strategic growth drivers, including creating value through innovation and expanding global reach, which positions the company well to address the needs and challenges of local markets. The decentralized management approach allows for operational flexibility and resource allocation to innovate and execute strategic plans. In 2015, the company invested $9.0 billion, or 12.9% of sales, in research and development, reflecting its commitment to delivering innovative products and services to meet evolving health care needs. The company’s diverse businesses, with more than 250 operating companies located in 60 countries, are key drivers of success, enabling it to adapt to changing market conditions and maintain operational continuity. The company strives to maintain a purpose-driven organization and is committed to developing global business leaders who can achieve growth objectives. The restructuring actions announced in the medical devices segment are expected to result in annualized pre-tax cost savings, providing added flexibility and resources to fund investment in new growth opportunities. The company continues to monitor economic challenges in various regions and takes appropriate actions to mitigate risks associated with external pressures and market volatility. The ability to foresee and confront sudden disruptive changes is reflected in the company’s strategic initiatives and resilience strategies, which are designed to sustain market leadership positions and enable growth. The company’s operational management practices emphasize the importance of organizational health and adaptability in the face of adversity, ensuring that it can maintain performance under pressure. The management believes that its foundational values, strategic framework, and long-term growth drivers will enable Johnson & Johnson to continue being a leader in the health care industry. The company also recognizes the need for effective crisis management and preparedness to navigate complex situations and environmental disturbances, which are integral to its operational resilience. The commitment to innovation and excellence in execution is essential for sustaining competitive advantage and achieving major objectives in a rapidly evolving market landscape.
