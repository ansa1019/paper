The COVID-19 pandemic has negatively impacted the global economy, disrupted global supply chains, and created significant volatility and disruption of financial markets. The full extent of the impact of the COVID-19 pandemic on operations and financial performance will depend on future developments, including the duration and spread of the pandemic, all of which are uncertain and cannot be predicted at this time. In mid-March 2020, certain customers began adjusting their service levels, which included a decrease in the frequency of pickups or a temporary pause in service. As service levels decreased, there was also a decrease in certain variable costs of operations. This decline in service activity peaked in the first half of April 2020 and improved sequentially through December 31, 2021. In April 2020, the company launched the "Committed to Serve" initiative, committing $20 million to support frontline employees and their families, as well as small business customers in local communities. The company has experienced an increase in certain costs of doing business as a direct result of the COVID-19 pandemic, including costs for additional safety equipment and hygiene products. These costs are intended to assist in protecting the safety of frontline employees while continuing to provide essential services to customers. The company recognized frontline employees for their commitment and contributions to their communities during the pandemic with awards. The effects of the COVID-19 pandemic on business are described in more detail in the results of operations discussion. The acquisition of US Ecology on February 8, 2022, is intended to enhance the company's environmental solutions capabilities, reflecting a strategic initiative to adapt to changing market conditions and customer needs. The company remains focused on executing its strategy to deliver consistent earnings and free cash flow growth while partnering with customers to create a more sustainable world. The guidance for 2022 includes a focus on driving profitable growth, making disciplined acquisition investments, and advancing technology to drive operational excellence. The company is committed to maintaining an efficient capital structure and preserving investment-grade credit ratings. An extended period of economic disruption associated with the COVID-19 pandemic could further disrupt the global supply chain, negatively impact demand for services, and disrupt financial markets. The company will continue to monitor the evolving COVID-19 pandemic along with its effects on business and access to capital markets. The credit facility allows for increased availability through commitments from existing lenders or the addition of new lenders, which supports operational flexibility and resilience in the face of external pressures. The company’s operational management includes strategies to mitigate risks associated with environmental liabilities and to ensure compliance with regulatory requirements, which are critical for maintaining organizational health and resilience. The company’s financial resources and strategic planning efforts are aimed at ensuring operational continuity and adaptability in response to unforeseen challenges, thereby supporting long-term organizational success and resilience.
