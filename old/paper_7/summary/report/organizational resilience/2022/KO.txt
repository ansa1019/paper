The Coca-Cola Company operates in a highly competitive commercial beverage industry, facing strong competition from numerous other general and specialty beverage companies. The company is impacted by various factors, including the cost to manufacture and distribute products, consumer spending, economic conditions, availability and quality of water, consumer preferences, inflation, geopolitical conditions, local and national laws and regulations, foreign currency exchange rate fluctuations, fuel prices, weather patterns, and the COVID-19 pandemic. The company announced the suspension of its business in Russia due to the conflict between Russia and Ukraine, which has disrupted operations in Ukraine. The company continues to monitor and assess the situation as circumstances evolve. Throughout 2022, the effects of the COVID-19 pandemic, including the resurgence of the virus in certain countries and related governmental actions, negatively impacted the business. Despite uncertainties caused by the pandemic and factors such as supply chain states, labor shortages, and inflation, the company is focused on executing for growth and believes it is well positioned to create value for stakeholders. The company is committed to investing in its portfolio of brands, strategic capabilities, and people, focusing on unlocking the potential of its portfolio, developing a robust innovation pipeline, increasing consumer-centric marketing effectiveness, and further embedding sustainability goals into operations. The company recognizes that evolving consumer product preferences and shifting demographics represent significant challenges, and it is committed to meeting changing consumer needs through an expanded portfolio of beverage choices, innovative packaging, and ingredient education efforts. The company also faces challenges related to product safety and quality, striving to meet the highest standards and addressing consumer concerns regarding ingredients. The company’s sustainability focus areas include water quality and scarcity, packaging materials, reduction of carbon emissions, sustainable sourcing, and diversity, equity, and inclusion. The company acknowledges that achieving its sustainability goals depends on its actions and those of various stakeholders, including bottling partners and suppliers. The company’s organizational structure consists of several operating segments, and it engages in buying and selling ownership interests in bottling partners and other operations to improve performance. The company measures unit case volume growth as a key performance metric, reflecting demand for its products at the consumer level. In 2022, the company sold 32.7 billion unit cases of its products, an increase driven by strength in away-from-home channels and ongoing investments. The company’s net operating revenues increased by 11 percent, attributed to volume growth, pricing initiatives, and favorable product and geographic mix. The company’s operational flexibility and strategic initiatives are crucial for navigating external pressures and maintaining performance under challenging conditions. The company actively manages its financial resources and liquidity position, utilizing debt financing to support operations and investments while ensuring resilience against economic fluctuations. The company’s ability to adapt to changing market conditions and consumer preferences is essential for sustaining its competitive advantage and ensuring long-term organizational resilience.
