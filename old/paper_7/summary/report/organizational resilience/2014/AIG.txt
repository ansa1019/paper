This annual report on Form 10-K discusses various aspects of American International Group, Inc. (AIG) that relate to organizational resilience. The company emphasizes the importance of maintaining operational flexibility and adaptability in the face of external pressures and disruptive events. AIG's enterprise risk management framework is designed to ensure that sufficient resources are available to meet obligations under both normal and stressed conditions, which reflects a commitment to resilience strategies. The organization actively monitors and manages risks, including credit, market, liquidity, and operational risks, to maintain organizational health and effectiveness. AIG's governance structure fosters a risk and control culture that encompasses all significant risk categories, with accountability for risk management aligned with corporate executives. 

The company employs a robust framework for measuring and managing risks, including the use of stress testing to assess the impact of potential adverse events on its financial strength. AIG's liquidity risk management framework establishes minimum liquidity requirements to protect long-term viability and ensure timely fulfillment of financial obligations. The organization recognizes the need for proactive measures in response to changing market conditions and potential liquidity shortfalls, which may arise from catastrophic losses or significant operational disruptions. 

AIG's insurance operations are structured to achieve an acceptable risk-adjusted return on equity, with a focus on disciplined risk selection and premium adequacy. The company manages its insurance risks through exposure limitations, underwriting approval processes, and the use of reinsurance to mitigate potential losses. The establishment of reserves for unpaid losses and loss adjustment expenses is a critical component of AIG's risk management, with ongoing reviews to ensure adequacy in light of emerging claims experience. 

In the context of mortgage guaranty, AIG utilizes a proprietary risk quality index to manage the quality of loans insured, which reflects the organization’s ability to foresee and confront potential risks. The company’s commitment to service innovation and resource allocation supports its resilience in adapting to market fluctuations and customer needs. 

AIG’s operational management includes a focus on collaboration networks and inter-organizational cooperation to enhance resilience. The organization’s strategic initiatives are aimed at maintaining performance under pressure and ensuring operational continuity, particularly in the face of environmental disruptions and market volatility. 

The company’s approach to crisis management and preparedness is evident in its structured response mechanisms and the establishment of support systems to address unexpected situations. AIG's emphasis on knowledge management and learning processes contributes to its organizational intelligence and capability to adapt to complex situations. Overall, AIG's multifaceted strategies and operational practices are aligned with the principles of organizational resilience, ensuring that it can navigate adversity and maintain positive adjustment under challenging conditions.
