UnitedHealth Group is a diversified health care company dedicated to helping people live healthier lives and making the health system work better for everyone. Through its diversified family of businesses, the company leverages core competencies in data analytics, health information, advanced technology, and clinical expertise. The health care market continues to change based on demographic shifts, new regulations, political forces, and both payer and patient expectations. Health plans and care providers are being called upon to work together to close gaps in care and improve overall care quality, improve the health of populations, and reduce costs. The company continues to advocate for actuarially sound rates that are commensurate with medical cost trends and remains dedicated to partnering with states committed to the long-term viability of their programs. The ongoing pressure on Medicare Advantage funding places importance on effective medical management and ongoing improvements in administrative efficiency. The company seeks to intensify its medical and operating cost management, make changes to the size and composition of care provider networks, and implement or increase member premiums. The health care spending growth is expected to continue due to various factors, including inflation, medical technology advancements, regulatory requirements, and demographic trends. The company’s operational flexibility is evident in its ability to adjust pricing strategies based on local market conditions, competitive dynamics, and regulatory considerations. The commercial risk market remains highly competitive, and the company expects broad-based competition to continue as the industry adapts to individual and employer needs amid reform changes. The delivery system and payment modernization initiatives reflect the company’s commitment to improving care quality and fostering collaboration among care providers. The company’s strategic initiatives include leveraging data and analytics to provide necessary information to close gaps in care and improve overall health outcomes. As of December 31, 2018, nearly 17 million people were served through aligned contractual arrangements, including full-risk, shared-risk, and performance incentive payment approaches. The company’s financial flexibility is supported by significant cash flows from operations, which are crucial for meeting short-term and long-term obligations while maintaining liquidity. The management of liquidity and financial position is conducted in the context of overall business strategy, with a focus on maintaining operational and financial flexibility. The company continues to evaluate opportunities to expand operations, which may include internal development of new products and acquisitions. The ability to respond effectively to environmental disruptions and transform itself to emerge stronger from challenging situations is a key aspect of the company’s resilience strategy. The company’s approach to risk management involves assessing and mitigating risks associated with medical costs, regulatory changes, and market dynamics, thereby enhancing its operational resilience. The organizational capabilities are further strengthened by a focus on collaborative networks and strategic partnerships that foster innovation and adaptability in a rapidly changing health care landscape.
