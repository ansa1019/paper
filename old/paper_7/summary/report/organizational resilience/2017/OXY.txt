Occidental's strategy focuses on delivering a unique shareholder value proposition through continual enhancements to its asset quality, organizational capability, and innovative technical applications that provide competitive advantages. The oil and gas segment emphasizes long-term value creation and leadership in health, safety, and the environment, benefiting from scale, technical expertise, and commercial and governmental collaboration. These attributes allow Occidental to bring additional production quickly to market, extend the life of older fields at lower costs, and provide low-cost growth opportunities with advanced technology. Occidental aims to maximize shareholder returns through consistent dividend growth and targeted production growth rates of 5 to 8 percent average per year over the long term, maintaining a strong balance sheet to secure business and enhance shareholder value. The company seeks to limit its financial and political risks and positions itself to thrive in both up- and down-cycle commodity price environments. Recent strategic initiatives have resulted in Occidental exiting non-core areas and strengthening its position in areas where it has a competitive advantage. The oil and gas segment implements its strategy primarily by focusing on cost-reduction efficiencies, improvement in new well productivity, and better base management to reduce total spend per barrel. Occidental's operations are characterized by a focus on long-term value creation, which includes the use of enhanced oil recovery techniques in mature fields and a disciplined approach to capital expenditures. The chemical segment's strategy is to be a low-cost producer to maximize cash flow generation, concentrating on the chlorovinyls chain to take advantage of economies of scale. The midstream and marketing segment strives to maximize realized value by optimizing the use of its transportation, storage, and terminal assets, providing access to domestic and international market alternatives. Occidental evaluates opportunities across the value chain to generate returns and minimize costs. The company’s operations are subject to significant volatility due to various market conditions, and it continually adjusts capital expenditures in line with current economic conditions to keep returns above its cost of capital. Occidental's ability to add reserves depends on the success of improved recovery, extension, and discovery projects, which are influenced by reservoir characteristics, technology improvements, and oil and natural gas prices. The company maintains a focus on operational excellence and strategic initiatives that enhance its organizational resilience, enabling it to absorb strain and preserve functioning despite adversity. Occidental’s strategic planning includes a focus on future-oriented initiatives, resource allocation, and maintaining operational flexibility to adapt to changing market conditions. The company’s commitment to health and safety, environmental stewardship, and social responsibility further supports its resilience strategies, ensuring it can confront and recover from unexpected situations and pressures in the business environment.
