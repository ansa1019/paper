The risks described could materially and adversely affect our business, financial condition, and results of operations, including disruptions in our depot operations that could adversely affect sales and member satisfaction. We depend on the orderly operation of the merchandise receiving and distribution process, and unforeseen disruptions in operations due to catastrophic events may result in delays in the delivery of merchandise to our warehouses. Failure to adequately update our systems and disruptions in both our primary and back-up systems could harm our ability to run our business and adversely affect our results of operations. We believe that our change management process can mitigate the risk of system disruption, although excessive technological change could impact the effectiveness of adoption. Our ability to respond effectively to competitive pressures, changes in the retail markets, and member expectations is critical to maintaining our competitive position. We may be required to suspend operations in some or all of our locations due to public health issues, which could have a material adverse effect on our business. Natural disasters could negatively affect our operations and financial performance, resulting in physical damage to properties and disruptions in the supply of products. We are subject to risks associated with legislative, judicial, accounting, regulatory, political, and economic factors specific to the countries in which we operate, which could adversely affect our business. Our financial performance depends heavily on our ability to control costs, and we must attract, train, and retain a large and growing number of qualified employees while controlling related labor costs. Our investments in merchandise pricing can include reducing prices on merchandise to drive sales, which may negatively impact near-term gross margin. We are also subject to payment-related risks, including potential disruptions in payment transaction processing services. Our success depends on our ability to identify and respond to trends in demographics and consumer preferences, and failure to do so could negatively affect our relationship with our members. We are making technology investments to improve or replace our information processes and systems that are key to managing our business, and failure to monitor and choose the right investments could result in the loss of our competitive position. We are subject to various environmental regulations that could adversely impact our business, and any failure to comply with these laws could result in significant costs. Our ability to maintain positive membership loyalty and brand recognition is essential to our business model, and damage to our brands or reputation may negatively impact our sales and membership fee revenue. We face strong competition from other retailers and warehouse club operators, which could adversely affect our business. Our growth strategy depends on our ability to acquire property and build or lease new warehouses, and local regulations may impact our ability to find suitable locations. We are also exposed to fluctuations in foreign exchange rates that may adversely affect our results of operations. The risks associated with entering new markets include difficulties in attracting members and competition with established competitors. Our financial performance is influenced by general economic factors, including inflation, unemployment, and changes in consumer confidence, which could adversely affect demand for our products and services.
