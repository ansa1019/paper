The risks and uncertainties described could materially and adversely affect our business, financial condition, and results of operations. Strong competition could adversely affect prices and demand for our products and services and could decrease our market share. Our ability to be competitive on delivery times and delivery costs depends on many factors, including the success of our investments in supply chain management. Intense competitive pressures could affect our prices, margins, or demand for our products and services. We may not timely identify or effectively respond to consumer needs, expectations, or trends, which could adversely affect our relationship with customers, our reputation, and market share. The success of our business depends in part on our ability to identify and respond promptly to evolving trends in demographics, consumer preferences, and unexpected weather conditions or natural disasters. If we do not successfully differentiate the shopping experience to meet the individual needs and expectations of our customer groups, we may lose market share. We must continually anticipate and adapt to changes in the purchasing process, including customer expectations for delivery options. Our investments in our stores may not deliver the relevant shopping experience our customers expect, and we must maintain a safe store environment for our customers and associates. Failure to maintain our stores and to effectively implement localized merchandising assortments could adversely affect our business. Our ability to attract, develop, and retain highly qualified associates while controlling labor costs is critical to meeting customer expectations for service and product knowledge. A failure of a key information technology system could significantly disrupt our operations. Disruptions in our customer-facing technology systems could impair our interconnected retail strategy and negatively affect customer experiences. The implementation of our interconnected retail and technology initiatives could disrupt our operations in the near term. Disruptions in our supply chain could adversely impact our ability to deliver inventory in a timely manner, resulting in lost sales or damage to our reputation. Our business involves the receipt, storage, and transmission of sensitive information, and any significant compromise of our data security could result in substantial costs and reputational damage. Uncertainty regarding the housing market and economic conditions could adversely affect demand for our products and services. Our ability to effectively manage and expand our alliances and relationships with suppliers is critical to executing our strategy. Failure to achieve and maintain a high level of product and service quality could damage our image and negatively impact sales. We are subject to various legal and regulatory risks that could increase our costs and impact our operations. Our strategic transactions involve risks that could adversely impact our financial condition and results of operations. Changes in laws and regulations could increase our costs of doing business. Our ability to successfully conduct operations in international markets is affected by unique costs and difficulties. The inflation or deflation of commodity prices could affect demand for our products and profit margins. We are predominantly self-insured for various risks, and significant claims could adversely impact our financial condition. Changes in accounting standards could significantly affect our financial results. We are involved in legal proceedings that could adversely affect our operations or increase our costs.
