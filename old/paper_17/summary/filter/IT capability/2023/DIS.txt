The Walt Disney Company recognizes that information technology (IT) capabilities have been evolving in scope to encompass the coordination of the firm’s assets and internal processes, as well as their interfirm transactions and relationships. IT capability is defined as the firm's ability to acquire, deploy, combine, and reconfigure IT resources to support and enhance business strategies and work processes. The company emphasizes the importance of mobilizing and deploying IT-based resources in conjunction with leveraging the value of other resources and capabilities. The IT function’s competence is crucial in transforming the firm into a competitive force, with IT capabilities representing the application of both physical and intangible IT resources, such as technology, knowledge, practices, relationships, management skills, and human resources, to further organizational goals. The board of directors includes members with significant experience in technology and innovation, which informs the strategic direction of the company. For instance, the chief executive officer of Oracle Corporation provides insights into the rapidly changing technological landscape that affects Disney's businesses, while other board members bring expertise in digital transformation and the management of technology resources. The company’s governance practices ensure that strategic resource allocation aligns with business needs and market dynamics, promoting operational efficiency and flexibility. The audit committee oversees cybersecurity and data security risks, ensuring that the company’s IT governance framework is robust. The company’s strategic management practices incorporate performance metrics that link executive compensation to financial performance, including adjusted segment operating income and return on invested capital. The compensation committee has adopted policies that emphasize the importance of diversity and inclusion, creativity and innovation, and collaboration on strategic priorities, which are essential for driving company growth through technology and operational efficiencies. The company also recognizes the significance of external influences and market dynamics in shaping its IT capabilities, as it navigates challenges and opportunities in a competitive landscape. The integration of technology into business processes is critical for enhancing customer relationships and achieving market agility. The company’s commitment to innovation is evident in its strategic investments in digital infrastructure and technology deployment, which support the overall business strategy and enhance organizational effectiveness. The board’s governance guidelines reflect a commitment to aligning executive compensation with long-term shareholder value creation, ensuring that IT capabilities are leveraged to optimize performance outcomes and drive business transformation. The company’s approach to IT investment is guided by a resource-based view, focusing on the strategic alignment of technology with organizational goals and the dynamic capabilities necessary to respond to changing market conditions.
