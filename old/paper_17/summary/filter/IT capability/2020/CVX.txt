Chevron's operations are subject to various risks, including those related to information technology (IT) systems and cybersecurity. The company has experienced cyber incidents that could compromise its process control networks or other critical systems, resulting in disruptions to business operations, injury to people, harm to the environment, or loss of critical data and proprietary information. Cyber threat actors are becoming more sophisticated in their attempts to access the company's IT systems, including those of cloud providers and other third parties. Despite significant resources devoted to preventing intrusions, the company acknowledges that it will continue to experience varying degrees of cyber incidents. The reliance on third-party IT systems increases exposure to cyber incidents that could materially impact business operations. The company's risk management systems are designed to assess potential risks to its operations and assets, including those associated with IT capabilities. Effective management of these risks is crucial for maintaining operational efficiency and achieving organizational goals. The impact of changing commodity prices, influenced by technological advancements and market dynamics, is evaluated as a core part of Chevron's business planning process. Additionally, the company recognizes the importance of leveraging IT capabilities to enhance its business strategies and operational processes. The integration of digital technologies and the ability to provide reliable information management are essential for responding to evolving business needs. The company's ability to navigate the complexities of the market and maintain competitive advantage is closely tied to its IT capabilities and the effective deployment of technology resources. Furthermore, the ongoing evolution of IT capabilities underscores the necessity for Chevron to mobilize and deploy IT-based resources in conjunction with other strategic resources to support business transformation and enhance firm performance. The company is also aware of the potential impacts of environmental factors and regulatory changes on its operations, which may necessitate adjustments in its IT infrastructure and governance practices. As Chevron continues to adapt to the changing landscape, the alignment of its IT capabilities with business strategies will be critical for sustaining operational flexibility and achieving long-term performance excellence.
