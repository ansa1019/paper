The Walt Disney Company's IT capabilities have evolved to encompass the coordination of the firm's assets and internal processes, as well as interfirm transactions and relationships. The company emphasizes the importance of mobilizing and deploying IT-based resources alongside leveraging the value of other resources and capabilities to enhance business strategies and work processes. The ability to provide data and information with appropriate levels of accuracy, timeliness, reliability, security, confidentiality, connectivity, and access is critical in responding to changing business needs and directions. The company recognizes that its IT capabilities play a key role in transforming Disney into a competitive force, representing the application of both physical and intangible IT resources such as technology, knowledge, practices, relationships, and management skills to further organizational goals. The board of directors and executive officers possess extensive experience in evaluating operational, investment, and branding strategies, which aligns with Disney's strategic focus on innovation and technology integration. The leadership team is committed to building long-term value for shareholders, which involves significant investments in media and entertainment distribution businesses, as well as the development of digital platforms. The company has successfully launched several direct-to-consumer initiatives, resulting in substantial growth in subscriber numbers across its streaming services. The audit committee oversees cybersecurity and data security risks, ensuring that the company maintains robust IT governance practices. The integration of technology into business processes is viewed as essential for operational efficiency and organizational effectiveness. The company is focused on enhancing its digital infrastructure and capabilities to respond to market dynamics and consumer demands effectively. The board's governance practices include evaluating the alignment of executive compensation with performance metrics that drive long-term shareholder value, reflecting the company's commitment to strategic resource allocation and performance improvement. The compensation committee has implemented measures to ensure that executive compensation is tied to both financial performance and strategic objectives, reinforcing the importance of IT capabilities in achieving these goals. The company actively engages in risk management practices to mitigate potential challenges associated with technology deployment and operational flexibility. The strategic management of technology resources is integral to Disney's overall business strategy, which aims to optimize performance outcomes and enhance competitive positioning in the market.
