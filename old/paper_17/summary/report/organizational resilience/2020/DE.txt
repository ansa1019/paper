The following risks are considered material to John Deere's business based upon current knowledge, information, and assumptions, and should be closely considered in conjunction with management's discussion and analysis. The COVID pandemic resulted in additional risks that could materially adversely affect John Deere's business, financial condition, results of operations, and cash flows. The pandemic has resulted in governments implementing numerous measures that have impacted the company's workforce and operations, as well as those of customers and suppliers. Restrictions on access to manufacturing facilities, supply chain disruptions, and increased costs could limit the company's ability to meet customer demand, adversely affecting its financial condition. The uncertainties related to the pandemic may significantly affect the company's business and outlook, including disruptions in the supply chain and the company's ability to meet commitments to customers. John Deere's ability to adapt in highly competitive markets could affect its business, results of operations, and financial condition. The company competes on the basis of product performance, innovation, quality, distribution, customer service, and price, and its ability to understand customers' specific preferences is critical to its success. Changes in the availability and price of raw materials and components could result in significant disruptions to the supply chain and production, adversely affecting the company's ability to meet commitments to customers. Supply chain disruptions due to various factors could affect John Deere's operations and profitability. The occurrence of security breaches and disruptions to the company's information technology infrastructure could interfere with operations and compromise sensitive information, potentially damaging the company's reputation. John Deere's ability to attract, develop, engage, and retain qualified employees is essential for executing its strategy. The company's operations may be impacted by the loss of employees, particularly when departures involve groups, which could adversely affect morale and the company's ability to execute its business strategy. Unfavorable weather conditions or natural calamities that reduce agricultural production could directly and indirectly affect demand for John Deere's products. Additionally, increasingly rigorous environmental regulations could impose significant costs and affect the company's operations. The ability to respond effectively to environmental disruptions and transform itself to emerge stronger from challenging situations is crucial for maintaining operational continuity. John Deere's efforts to grow its businesses depend on access to additional geographic markets and the ability to adapt to changing conditions in various regions. The company's strategic initiatives may be impacted by geopolitical uncertainties, trade restrictions, and economic conditions, which could affect customer confidence and demand. The company's financial services segment is exposed to risks that could materially impact operations and financial results, including credit losses and changes in interest rates. Sustained increases in funding obligations under the company's pension plans may impair liquidity or financial condition. The company's ability to execute its business strategies in production systems and precision technologies could affect its results of operations and financial condition. The evolving landscape of privacy and data protection regulations presents additional challenges that could adversely affect the company's business.
