We face a variety of risks that may affect our operations, financial results, or stock price, many of which are driven by factors that we cannot control or predict. The challenging economic environment triggered by the coronavirus pandemic has impacted our business, results of operations, and financial condition. The pandemic has adversely impacted the economic environment, leading to lower interest rates and heightened volatility in the financial markets. Additionally, we have experienced significant increases in client cash balances, which have affected our tier 1 leverage ratio and limited our ability to return excess capital to stockholders. Our client service response and processing times have increased due to high levels of client engagement, and we have occasionally experienced delays accessing our website and mobile applications. Many employees are working remotely, which has contributed to increased response times. The timely achievement of our integration milestones has become more challenging due to the pandemic, particularly regarding technology and systems. The extent to which the pandemic impacts our business will depend on future developments, which are highly uncertain, including the scope and duration of the outbreak and actions taken by governmental authorities. Our business can be adversely affected by the general environment, including economic, corporate, regulatory, and geopolitical developments. We have established a comprehensive risk management program to identify and manage risks, including operational, compliance, credit, market, and liquidity risks. Our risk management process includes risk identification and assessment, risk measurement, risk monitoring, and reporting. We maintain a backup and recovery infrastructure to minimize business interruptions and ensure operational continuity during incidents. Our operational risk management includes specific policies and procedures to identify and manage risks arising from people, processes, systems, and external events. We continuously monitor our systems for potential threats and have designed an information security program to protect client accounts and data. We also manage fraud risk through policies and controls to prevent, detect, and report fraudulent activity. Our liquidity policies support the successful execution of business strategies while ensuring ongoing liquidity to meet operational needs. We conduct regular liquidity stress testing to develop a consolidated view of liquidity risk exposures. Our capital management strategy includes maintaining sufficient capital levels to absorb unanticipated losses and support balance sheet growth. We have adopted a policy to remain well capitalized even in stressed scenarios, and we conduct regular capital stress testing to assess potential financial impacts of adverse events. Our integration of td ameritrade includes aligning risk exposures with our risk appetite and evaluating new risks impacting the combined company. We expect to realize annualized cost synergies through integration and continue to incur significant acquisition and integration-related costs. The integration process may result in unforeseen expenditures and challenges, including employee retention and technology integration. We have established risk metrics and reporting to measure the impact of strategy execution against our risk appetite, and senior management actively participates in the risk management process.
