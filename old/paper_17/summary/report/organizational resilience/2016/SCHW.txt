The company faces a variety of risks that may affect its operations, financial results, or stock price, driven by factors beyond its control. Developments in the business, economic, and geopolitical environment could negatively impact the company's business. The company's operations are highly dependent on the integrity and resiliency of its critical business functions and technology systems. To minimize business interruptions, the company maintains a backup and recovery infrastructure, including facilities for backup and communications, a geographically dispersed workforce, and routine testing of business continuity and disaster recovery plans. Despite the company's efforts to identify areas of risk and implement risk management policies and procedures, there can be no assurance that the company will not suffer unexpected losses due to operational risks arising from inadequate or failed internal processes, people, and systems or from external events. The company has established a comprehensive risk management program to identify and manage these risks, which includes risk identification and assessment, risk measurement, risk monitoring and reporting, and risk mitigation. The global risk committee, comprised of senior executives, oversees risk management, including identifying emerging risks and assessing risk management practices. The company has developed policies and procedures to manage operational risk, including the use of periodic risk and control self-assessments and internal audit reviews to evaluate the effectiveness of internal controls. The company also faces information security risks, which are increasing due to the sophistication of cyber threats. The company continuously monitors its systems and collaborates with government agencies and other financial institutions to address potential threats. The company maintains policies and procedures regarding the standard of care expected with company data and provides regular training on data security. The company has a comprehensive capital contingency plan to provide action plans for low probability/high impact capital events, ensuring it remains well-capitalized even in stressed scenarios. The company conducts regular capital stress testing to assess potential financial impacts from adverse macroeconomic events. The company's liquidity needs are primarily driven by client activity and capital needs of its subsidiaries. It has established liquidity policies to support business strategies while ensuring sufficient liquidity to meet operational needs. The company employs a variety of methodologies to monitor and manage liquidity, including regular liquidity stress testing. The company has established limits and contingency funding scenarios to support liquidity levels during expected and stressed scenarios. The company also relies on external funding sources, including credit facilities and commercial paper programs, to meet its liquidity needs. The company is subject to extensive regulation, and compliance risks are managed through policies and procedures designed to achieve compliance with applicable legal and regulatory requirements. Despite these efforts, legal breaches and rule violations could result in reputational harm and significant losses. The company’s risk management framework incorporates its purpose, vision, and values, establishing a culture that emphasizes the importance of risk management in achieving its strategic objectives. The company’s operational flexibility and adaptability are critical in navigating environmental perturbations and ensuring business continuity.
