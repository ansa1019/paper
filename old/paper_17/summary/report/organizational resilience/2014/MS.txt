The company’s risk management and monitoring processes seek to quantify and mitigate risk to more extreme market moves. However, severe market events have historically been difficult to predict, and significant losses could occur if extreme market events were to happen. The company is subject to operational risks, including a failure, breach, or other disruption of its operational or security systems, which could adversely affect its businesses or reputation. The company maintains extensive controls to reduce the risk of incorrect valuation or risk management of its trading positions due to flaws in data, models, systems, or processes. Despite the business contingency plans in place, there can be no assurance that such plans will fully mitigate all potential business continuity risks. The company’s ability to conduct business may be adversely affected by disruptions in the infrastructure that supports its business and the communities where it operates, which may include disruptions involving physical site access, cyber incidents, or natural disasters. The company has established an operational risk framework to identify, measure, monitor, and control risk across its operations, with primary responsibility for managing operational risk resting with the business segments and control groups. The operational risk department provides oversight and independently assesses, measures, and monitors operational risk. The company’s operational risk management framework is continually evolving to account for changes in the business and respond to the changing regulatory environment. The company has implemented operational risk data and assessment systems to monitor and analyze internal and external operational risk events, which are incorporated into the operational risk capital model. The company’s liquidity risk management framework aims to ensure access to adequate funding across a wide range of market conditions, with a contingency funding plan anticipating periods of limited access to funding. The company maintains a global liquidity reserve to cover daily funding needs and to meet strategic liquidity targets. The company’s risk exposure is managed through diversification of funding sources and alignment of the maturity profile of assets and liabilities. The company’s risk management strategies may not be fully effective in mitigating risk exposures in all market environments or against all types of risk. The company’s risk management philosophy emphasizes the pursuit of risk-adjusted returns through prudent risk-taking that protects its capital base. The company has established a culture of effective risk management through its defined core values, governance framework, and ongoing training and development programs. The company’s operational risk management includes a focus on business continuity management, which identifies key risks and threats to the company’s resiliency and plans to ensure recovery strategies are in place for critical business functions following a disaster or other business interruption. The company’s operational risk department works with business segments to ensure a comprehensive framework for managing operational risk, including cybersecurity and vendor risk oversight. The company’s ability to adapt to unexpected situations and maintain operational flexibility is critical for its resilience in the face of environmental perturbations and other external pressures.
