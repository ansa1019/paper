Citi reported balanced operating results for full-year 2017, reflecting continued momentum across businesses and geographies, notably in areas where investments have been made. Despite various economic, political, and other risks and uncertainties that could impact Citi's businesses and future results, the company intends to build on the progress made during 2017, focusing on optimizing performance to benefit shareholders. The results included an updated estimate for a one-time, non-cash charge related to tax reform, which impacted the tax line within corporate/other and the tax lines in North America Global Consumer Banking (GCB) and Institutional Clients Group (ICG). Citi's end-of-period loans increased 7% to $667 billion, with a 9% growth in ICG and a 4% growth in GCB, demonstrating operational flexibility and resilience in resource deployment despite the wind-down of legacy assets. Operating expenses were largely unchanged year-over-year, as higher volume-related expenses and ongoing investments were offset by efficiency savings. The increase in provisions for credit losses was primarily driven by volume growth and seasoning, particularly in North America cards portfolios, indicating a proactive approach to risk assessment and management.
