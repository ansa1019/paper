Citi demonstrated continued progress across the franchise during 2021, reflecting a significant allowance for credit loss release of approximately $8.8 billion due to improvements in the macroeconomic environment and portfolio credit quality. The company continued to invest in its transformation, including infrastructure supporting its risk and control environment, which is essential for maintaining operational flexibility and resilience under challenging conditions. Various macroeconomic challenges and uncertainties, such as the duration and severity of the pandemic-related public health crisis, disruptions of global supply chains, inflationary pressures, and geopolitical tensions, will continue to create uncertainty around Citi's businesses and results, highlighting the need for effective risk mitigation and adaptability. Citi's planned divestitures of its consumer businesses across Mexico, Asia, and EMEA are aligned with the repositioning of its consumer operations to focus on global wealth centers, reflecting a strategic response to external pressures and a commitment to organizational health and sustainability. The company reported net income of $22.0 billion, driven by lower cost of credit, while revenues decreased 5% from the prior year, indicating a need for ongoing strategic initiatives to enhance performance under pressure. Citi's operating expenses increased 9%, primarily due to investments in transformation and business-led initiatives, which are critical for maintaining resilience and operational continuity. The net allowance for credit losses reflected an improvement in portfolio credit quality and the macroeconomic outlook, emphasizing the importance of knowledge absorption and proactive measures in risk assessment. The company’s capital ratios remained robust, with a common equity tier 1 capital ratio of 12.2%, demonstrating financial resilience amidst economic fluctuations. Citi's institutional clients group reported a net income increase of 36%, reflecting lower cost of credit and continued investments in transformation, which are vital for sustaining competitive advantage and operational effectiveness. The global consumer banking segment faced challenges with revenues decreasing 10%, yet it maintained strong deposit growth, showcasing the ability to adapt to changing market conditions. The company’s focus on enhancing its risk and control environment, alongside the establishment of oversight committees, underscores its commitment to organizational resilience and effective crisis management. The ongoing transformation efforts are designed to strengthen Citi's business and operating models, ensuring that it can confront future uncertainties and maintain positive adjustment in the face of adversity.
