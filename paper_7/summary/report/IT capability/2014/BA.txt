Boeing’s discussion emphasizes its coordination of assets and processes across a global network, noting that “we conduct operations in many countries and rely on an extensive network of international partners, key suppliers and subcontractors” to support its commercial and defense businesses and to leverage resources for growth and productivity; it also highlights ongoing efforts “to find new ways to improve efficiency and quality” as core to delivering superior design and value. In its fleet support services, which “provide the operators of our commercial airplanes with assistance and services to facilitate efficient and safe airplane operation,” Boeing describes a comprehensive information management capability that “includes flight and maintenance training, field service support costs, engineering services, information services and systems and technical data and documents” to furnish accurate, timely and reliable data and guidance, thereby mobilizing and deploying technology-based resources in combination with other organizational assets to enhance operational flexibility, customer satisfaction and competitive performance.
