The Company’s filings highlight its ongoing emphasis on information technology–enabled capabilities, noting “the company’s ability to develop and launch new products, services and capabilities in a timely and successful manner, including Schwab Intelligent Portfolios™”; affirming that “the company’s operations are highly dependent on the integrity of its technology systems and the company’s success depends, in part, on its ability to make timely enhancements and additions to its technology in anticipation of evolving client needs”; describing investments in “two data centers intended, in part, to further improve the recovery of business processing in the event of an emergency” and a commitment “to an ongoing process of upgrading, enhancing, and testing its technology systems”; reporting that professional services expense increased “primarily due to higher spending on technology services and an increase in fees paid to outsourced service providers and consultants” and that occupancy and equipment expense rose “primarily due to an increase in software maintenance expense relating to the company’s information technology systems”; disclosing that capital expenditures were “primarily for buildings and land relating to changes in the company’s geographic footprint, developing internal-use software, and software and equipment relating to the company’s information technology systems,” with “capitalized costs for developing internal-use software” of $81 million in 2014 and $74 million in 2013; detailing governance of technology risks through an information security and privacy sub-committee that “provides oversight of the information security and privacy programs and policies,” a model governance sub-committee that “provides oversight of model risk throughout the company,” and a vendor management sub-committee that “provides oversight of the company’s vendor management and outsourcing program and policies”; explaining that “the company maintains policies and procedures and technology to protect against fraud and unauthorized access to systems and data,” “maintains policies and procedures regarding the standard of care expected with company data,” and delivers “regular training … in regard to data security”; describing operational risk as including “computer viruses, cyber attacks, terrorist attacks, and natural disaster” and noting that “the company maintains backup and recovery functions, including facilities for backup and communications, and conducts periodic testing of disaster recovery plans”; and stating that “all models at the company are registered in a centralized database and classified into different risk ratings depending on their potential financial, reputational, or regulatory impact to the company,” while the company “manages its exposure to external vendor risk through contractual provisions, control standards, and ongoing monitoring of vendor performance.”
