Corporate functions at Procter & Gamble include information technology among company-level services alongside strategy and portfolio analysis, corporate accounting, treasury, tax, external relations, governance and legal services, and global business services provides scaled services in technology, process and data tools to enable the sector business units, the enterprise markets and corporate functions to better serve consumers and customers. The MD&A identifies as a key risk “the ability to rely on and maintain key company and third-party information and operational technology systems, networks and services and maintain the security and functionality of such systems, networks and services and the data contained therein.” The company’s strategic focus expressly includes increasing digital acumen to drive consumer and customer preference, reduce cost and enable rapid and efficient decision making, and P&G reports it is “constructively disrupting … how we innovate, communicate and leverage new technologies” to create more value. The report further highlights sales through e-commerce (including social commerce) channels, reflecting the coordination of digital technologies with traditional retail execution and the broader value chain.
