Tesla’s management disclosed that the Company is increasingly focused on products and services based on artificial intelligence, robotics and automation; in the fourth quarter of 2022, it recognized $324 million in revenue related to the general Full Self-Driving feature release in North America. Automotive sales revenue includes performance obligations for access to FSD features, internet connectivity, the supercharger network and over-the-air software updates, with related revenues recognized upon delivery or over time, and both cost of automotive sales revenue and cost of automotive leasing revenue include vehicle connectivity costs and allocations of electricity and infrastructure costs related to the free supercharging programs and supercharger network. The Company also manages significant holdings in digital assets, which are considered indefinite-lived intangible assets under GAAP and may be increased or decreased at any time based on business needs and market and environmental conditions, with impairment losses or gains recorded in restructuring and other expense. These disclosures reflect Tesla’s deployment and coordination of digital technologies, connectivity infrastructure and information delivery capabilities to support its business strategies, operational processes and competitive positioning.
