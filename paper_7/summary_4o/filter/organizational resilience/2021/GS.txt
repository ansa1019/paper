In the management's discussion and analysis, the Goldman Sachs Group, Inc. emphasizes the importance of business continuity planning (BCP) strategies, which are critical for maintaining operational resilience during disruptive events. The firm has activated its BCP strategy in response to the COVID-19 pandemic, focusing on safeguarding employees and ensuring continuity of business operations. The organization has established a comprehensive framework for assessing, monitoring, and managing operational risks, which includes the firmwide operational risk and resilience committee responsible for overseeing operational risk and ensuring business and operational resilience. This committee monitors threats and assesses risks to maintain readiness in the event of significant operational disruptions. The firm has maintained high liquidity levels, averaging $335 billion in global core liquid assets (GCLA) during 2021, which supports its ability to meet potential cash and collateral needs during a liquidity crisis. The liquidity risk management framework includes holding sufficient excess liquidity to cover outflows during stressed periods and maintaining appropriate asset-liability management. The firm conducts various scenario analyses and stress tests to evaluate its liquidity position under adverse conditions, ensuring that it can continue to operate effectively even in challenging environments. The firm’s risk management processes are designed to identify, assess, and mitigate risks, including those arising from external pressures and operational failures. The organization also focuses on enhancing its operational capabilities through continuous improvement and adaptation strategies, ensuring that it can respond effectively to unexpected situations. The firm’s approach to risk management includes a strong emphasis on communication and collaboration across departments, fostering a culture of resilience and proactive measures to address potential risks. The firm’s ability to adapt to changing market conditions and maintain operational flexibility is crucial for sustaining performance and achieving strategic objectives. The organization’s resilience strategies are further supported by its commitment to innovation and resource allocation, which enable it to navigate complex situations and maintain competitive advantage. The firm recognizes the multifaceted nature of resilience, encompassing not only internal processes and systems but also the broader economic and regulatory environment in which it operates. Through these comprehensive measures, Goldman Sachs aims to ensure its organizational health and effectiveness, positioning itself for success in a dynamic and often turbulent business landscape.
