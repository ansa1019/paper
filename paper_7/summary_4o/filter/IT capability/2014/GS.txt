The Goldman Sachs Group, Inc. is a leading global investment banking, securities and investment management firm that provides a wide range of financial services to a substantial and diversified client base. The firm emphasizes the importance of mobilizing and deploying IT-based resources in combination with leveraging the value of other resources and capabilities. IT capability has evolved to encompass the coordination of a firm’s assets and internal processes, along with interfirm transactions and relationships. The firm’s IT capabilities represent the application of physical or intangible IT resources, including technology, knowledge, practices, relationships, management skills, and business process understanding, to further organizational goals. The firm actively manages its operational risk through a comprehensive control framework designed to minimize operational risks, which includes training, supervision, and development of personnel. The risk management framework is built around governance, processes, and people, with senior management participating in identifying and mitigating key operational risks across the firm. The firm also utilizes technology to monitor market risk, employing a variety of risk measures to estimate potential losses from market movements. These measures include value-at-risk (VaR) and stress testing, which are integral to the firm’s risk management processes. The firm’s operational risk management process incorporates qualitative and quantitative assessments of factors such as internal controls, complexity of business activities, and the legal and regulatory environment. The firm’s liquidity risk management policies ensure that it maintains sufficient liquidity to fund operations even in adverse conditions. The firm’s liquidity position is supported by its global core liquid assets, which are unencumbered, highly liquid securities and cash. The firm’s credit risk management process includes assessing the likelihood of counterparty defaults, measuring current and potential credit exposure, and utilizing credit risk mitigants such as collateral and hedging. The firm also conducts regular stress tests to evaluate the impact of adverse market conditions on its credit exposures. The firm’s investment management services and products are designed to meet the diverse needs of institutional and individual clients, and the firm continuously evaluates its technology capabilities to enhance operational efficiency and support business innovation. The firm’s strategic resource allocation is influenced by its assessment of market dynamics, competitive positioning, and regulatory context, ensuring alignment with its organizational goals and market responsiveness. The integration of technology into business processes is critical to maintaining competitive advantage and operational flexibility, enabling the firm to respond effectively to changing business needs and market conditions.
