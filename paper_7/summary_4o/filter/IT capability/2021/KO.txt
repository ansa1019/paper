The Coca-Cola Company operates in a highly competitive commercial beverage industry, facing strong competition from numerous other general and specialty beverage companies. The company recognizes that its success depends on its ability to connect with consumers by providing a wide variety of beverage options to meet their desires, needs, and lifestyles. The company has transformed into a networked global organization designed to combine the power of scale with the deep knowledge required to win locally, which underscores the importance of mobilizing and deploying IT-based resources in combination with leveraging the value of other resources and capabilities. Throughout the pandemic, business continuity and adapting to customer needs have been critical, leading to the development of systemwide knowledge-sharing routines and processes, including the management of supply chain challenges. The company has focused on unlocking the potential of its portfolio of strong global, regional, and scaled local brands, developing a robust innovation pipeline, and increasing consumer-centric marketing effectiveness and efficiency. The evolving competitive landscape and the growth of e-commerce have led to dramatic shifts in consumer shopping habits, necessitating continuous strengthening of capabilities in marketing and innovation to maintain brand loyalty and market share. The company has increased investments in e-commerce to support retail and meal delivery services and is shifting more consumer and trade promotions to digital platforms. The company strives to meet the highest standards in product safety and quality, ensuring consistent safety and quality through strong governance and compliance with applicable regulations and standards. The management has identified challenges and risks that demand attention, including evolving consumer product preferences, which are impacted by shifting demographics and needs, on-the-go lifestyles, and consumers who are empowered with more information than ever. The company is committed to meeting these needs and generating growth through its evolving portfolio of beverage brands and products, including innovative and sustainable packaging. The company’s organizational structure consists of various operating segments, and it continually engages in buying and selling ownership interests in bottling partners and other manufacturing operations to improve its operating performance. The company measures performance through unit case volume growth, which reflects demand for its products at the consumer level. The company’s ability to generate cash flows from operating activities is considered a fundamental strength, and it utilizes debt financing to lower its overall cost of capital. The company has also established specific ESG goals related to water quality, packaging materials, and reduction of carbon emissions, which are dependent on various stakeholders' actions. The Coca-Cola Company remains focused on long-term opportunities for growth while adapting to the ever-changing business environment, ensuring that its IT capabilities evolve to support and enhance its business strategies and operational efficiencies.
