The Coca-Cola Company recognizes that its success depends on its ability to connect with consumers by providing a wide variety of choices to meet their desires, needs, and lifestyle choices. The company believes that its formidable assets, including its brands, financial strength, unrivaled distribution system, global reach, and the talent and strong commitment of its management and associates, are critical to achieving long-term sustainable growth. The company is committed to enhancing its core capabilities, which include consumer marketing, commercial leadership, franchise leadership, and bottling and distribution operations. These capabilities are essential for adapting to evolving consumer preferences and addressing challenges such as increased competition and changing market conditions. The company faces various risks, including obesity, water quality and quantity, and food security, which necessitate a proactive approach to organizational resilience. The company has implemented a robust water stewardship and management program to address water-related risks and improve water use efficiency. Additionally, it is focused on developing innovative packaging and merchandising solutions to drive demand for its beverages and meet the growing needs of consumers. The company’s productivity and reinvestment program aims to strengthen its brands and reinvest resources to drive long-term profitable growth, targeting annualized productivity improvements. The company also emphasizes the importance of inter-organizational collaboration with bottling partners to achieve scale and efficiencies, share best practices, and produce differentiated beverages. Furthermore, the company is actively engaged in managing its financial resources, including maintaining a strong liquidity position and managing debt levels prudently. The company’s ability to generate cash from operating activities is considered one of its fundamental financial strengths, providing significant flexibility to meet financial commitments. The company recognizes the impact of foreign currency fluctuations on its operations and has a foreign currency management program designed to mitigate the effects of exchange rate changes on net income and earnings per share. The company’s organizational structure includes various operating segments, each contributing to its overall resilience and adaptability in the face of market challenges. The company continuously assesses its operational practices and strategic initiatives to ensure effective responses to external pressures and to maintain organizational health. The Coca-Cola Company is committed to leveraging its strengths and capabilities to navigate complex situations, ensuring its operational continuity and resilience in a competitive landscape.
