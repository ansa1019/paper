The Goldman Sachs Group, Inc. has emphasized the importance of mobilizing and deploying IT-based resources in combination with leveraging the value of other resources and capabilities to transform the firm into a competitive force. The firm’s IT capabilities represent the application of physical or intangible IT resources, such as technology, knowledge, practices, relationships, management skills, business process understanding, and human resources, to further organizational goals. The firm’s operational risk management framework is integrated into its overall risk management processes, which includes the establishment of a cybersecurity program designed to identify, assess, document, and mitigate threats, ensuring the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of data and systems. The firm also recognizes the increasing reliance on technology and third-party relationships, which has resulted in heightened operational risks, including cybersecurity risks. The cybersecurity risk management process is overseen by the Chief Information Security Officer and includes monitoring for known vulnerabilities and signs of unauthorized access. The firm employs a comprehensive data collection process for operational risk events, requiring all employees to report and escalate these events. The operational risk management framework is designed to comply with operational risk measurement rules under the capital framework and has evolved based on changing needs and regulatory guidance. The firm’s risk management governance structure includes oversight from the board and its committees, which review the firmwide risk portfolio, including operational and technology risks. The firm’s technology risk team assesses and manages material risks from cybersecurity threats and sets firmwide control requirements. The firm also conducts regular stress tests to calculate credit exposures and potential concentrations that would result from applying shocks to counterparty credit ratings or credit risk factors. The firm’s liquidity risk management principles include holding sufficient excess liquidity in the form of highly liquid securities and cash to cover outflows during a stressed period. The firm maintains a diversified funding profile with an appropriate tenor, taking into consideration the characteristics and liquidity profile of its assets. The firm’s capital management activities are overseen by the board and its committees, ensuring compliance with capital rules and related policies. The firm’s operational resilience framework ensures that critical functions can continue to operate in the event of a disruption, and it regularly tests response and recovery strategies. The firm’s approach to managing third-party risk includes conducting due diligence across multiple risk domains, including information security and cybersecurity. The firm’s risk management framework is designed to enable comprehensive risk management processes through which it identifies, assesses, monitors, and manages the risks it assumes in conducting its activities. The firm’s investment in technology and its governance framework are critical to maintaining its competitive advantage and operational efficiency in a rapidly evolving business environment.
