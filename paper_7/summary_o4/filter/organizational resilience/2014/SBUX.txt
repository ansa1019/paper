Starbucks discloses that it actively manages its cash and investments in order to internally fund operating needs, make scheduled interest and principal payments on its borrowings, and return cash to shareholders through dividends and share repurchases, thereby preserving operational continuity and financial flexibility; it employs fixed-price and price-to-be-fixed commodity contracts and derivative hedges for coffee, dairy and diesel purchases and foreign currency cash flows to mitigate price volatility and currency fluctuations, monitors and limits associated counterparty credit risk, and performs sensitivity analyses to assess potential impacts on net earnings and other comprehensive income; its unsecured revolving credit facility and registered senior notes carry covenants limiting liens and sale-leaseback transactions, supporting disciplined capital structure management and adaptability under external pressures; it plans approximately $1.4 billion in fiscal 2015 capital expenditures for new stores, renovations and systems and technology investments to support growth initiatives and changing market conditions; it leverages sales growth, cost management and a shift toward licensed stores—including disciplined expansion in EMEA and China/Asia Pacific—to drive revenue growth, expand operating margins and reconfigure its store portfolio in response to foreign exchange movements and consumer demand; and it believes future operating cash flows and existing domestic and offshore liquidity will be sufficient to finance its core business requirements and shareholder distributions for the foreseeable future, demonstrating its ability to absorb strain and preserve or improve performance under varying internal and external disturbances.
