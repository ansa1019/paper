The company’s disclosures emphasize its ability to maintain key manufacturing and supply arrangements (including execution of supply chain optimizations and sole supplier and sole manufacturing plant arrangements) and to manage disruption of business due to factors outside of its control, such as natural disasters, acts of war or terrorism, or disease outbreaks; its ability to successfully manage cost fluctuations and pressures, including prices of commodities and raw materials, and costs of labor, transportation, energy, pension and healthcare; its ability to stay on the leading edge of innovation, obtain necessary intellectual property protections and successfully respond to changing consumer habits and technological advances attained by competitors; its ability to successfully manage the financial, legal, reputational and operational risk associated with third-party relationships, such as suppliers, contract manufacturers, distributors, contractors and external business partners; its ability to rely on and maintain key company and third-party information and operational technology systems, networks and services, and maintain the security and functionality of such systems, networks and services and the data contained therein; its ability to successfully manage uncertainties related to changing political conditions (including the United Kingdom’s exit from the European Union) and potential implications such as exchange rate fluctuations and market contraction; its ability to successfully manage regulatory and legal requirements and matters; its ability to successfully manage ongoing acquisition, divestiture and joint venture activities to achieve overall business strategy and financial objectives without impacting delivery of base business objectives; its ability to successfully achieve productivity improvements and cost savings and manage ongoing organizational changes while identifying, developing and retaining key employees; and its ability to successfully manage the demand, supply and operational challenges associated with a disease outbreak, including epidemics, pandemics or similar widespread public health concerns (including the novel coronavirus, COVID-19, outbreak).  In responding to the COVID-19 pandemic, the company undertook government-recommended actions to protect employees worldwide, worked with local and national officials to keep manufacturing facilities open due to the essential nature of its products, broadly maintained operations despite some supply-chain disruptions caused by restricted employee movements and increased transportation and manufacturing costs, and intends to continue implementing safety measures to ensure ongoing manufacturing and distribution.  The company also negotiated extended payment terms with suppliers and implemented a supply-chain finance program enabling qualifying suppliers to sell receivables to banks, which together contributed to a $1.9 billion increase in operating cash flow in fiscal 2020.  Throughout these challenges, the company generated $17.4 billion of operating cash flow, invested $3.1 billion of capital expenditures to support capacity expansion, innovation and cost efficiencies, maintained access to commercial paper and bond markets through strong credit ratings and undrawn credit facilities, and sustained 130 consecutive years of dividend payments (including 64 consecutive years of increases).
