Berkshire Hathaway reports that it “manages our operating businesses on an unusually decentralized basis,” with “essentially no centralized or integrated business functions and minimal involvement by our corporate headquarters in the day-to-day business activities of the operating businesses,” a structure that implicitly relies on individual business units to coordinate assets, processes and external relationships. In its primary auto insurance operations, GEICO “markets its policies mainly by direct response methods where most customers apply for coverage directly to the company via the internet or over the telephone,” demonstrating the deployment of digital channels and connectivity to support customer access and transactional workflows.
