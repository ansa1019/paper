We adapt our monetization trends, for example by accommodating programmatic buying—which “presents opportunities for advertisers to connect with the right user, in the right moment, in the right context”—while managing fluctuations in traffic acquisition costs driven by device mix, partner agreement terms, and paid access points to mitigate margin pressure. As users in developing economies increasingly come online, we develop localized versions of our products and advertising programs and maintain “a foreign exchange risk management program designed to reduce our exposure” to currency fluctuations, thereby preserving revenues despite external disturbances. We diversify our revenue base by expanding non-advertising offerings such as Google Cloud, Google Play, hardware products and other bets, recognizing that sources of revenues “could change over time and the revenues could be volatile,” yet maintaining overall business continuity. We “invest heavily in research and development” and our capital expenditures fluctuate as we build systems, data centers, real estate and IT infrastructure, and pursue acquisitions that “enhance the breadth and depth of our offerings” and expand our expertise, reflecting strategic resource deployment to sustain innovation and operational flexibility under challenging conditions. We regard our employees as critical assets whose “energy and talent drive Alphabet and create our success,” and we expect to continue investing in talent globally to reinforce our organizational capabilities. Our robust liquidity framework—including cash, cash equivalents and marketable securities of $86.3 billion, a $5 billion commercial paper program, a $4 billion revolving credit facility, and senior notes—together with proactive financial and operational decision-making, underpins our capacity to maintain our integrity, performance and business continuity under internal and external variations and uncertainties.
