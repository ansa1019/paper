Procter & Gamble discloses its ability to manage disruptions in credit markets or to its banking partners or changes to its credit rating; to maintain key manufacturing and supply arrangements (including execution of supply chain optimizations and sole‐supplier and sole manufacturing plant arrangements) and to manage disruption of business due to factors outside its control such as natural disasters, acts of war (including the Russia–Ukraine war), terrorism or disease outbreaks; to manage cost fluctuations and pressures, including prices of commodities, raw materials, labor, transportation and energy; to stay on the leading edge of innovation, obtain necessary intellectual property protections and respond to changing consumer habits, evolving digital marketing and platform requirements and technological advances; to compete effectively in local and global sales channels by responding to competitive factors such as prices, promotional incentives and trade terms; to manage and maintain key customer relationships; to protect its reputation and brand equity by managing real or perceived issues related to safety, quality, supply chain practices or similar matters; to manage the financial, legal, reputational and operational risk associated with third-party relationships; to rely on and maintain key company and third-party information and operational technology systems, networks and services and maintain their security and functionality; to manage uncertainties related to changing political conditions, exchange rate fluctuations and market contractions; to manage current and expanding regulatory and legal requirements across product liability, packaging composition, intellectual property, labor and employment, antitrust, privacy and data protection, tax, environmental and financial reporting matters; to manage changes in applicable tax laws and regulations; to execute ongoing acquisition, divestiture and joint venture activities without impacting the delivery of base business objectives; to achieve productivity improvements and cost savings and manage ongoing organizational changes while identifying, developing and retaining key employees; to manage demand, supply and operational challenges and governmental mandates associated with epidemics, pandemics or similar public health concerns; to address uncertainties, sanctions and economic effects from the Russia–Ukraine war; and to achieve its ambition of reducing greenhouse gas emissions and making progress on environmental sustainability priorities. In support of these capabilities, the company acts with agility and constructively disrupts its industry and internal operating models—including how it innovates, communicates and leverages new technologies—to create value; improves operational effectiveness and organizational culture through enhanced clarity of roles and responsibilities, accountability and incentive compensation; works with suppliers and authorities to implement safety measures that minimize manufacturing and distribution disruptions; and is developing next-level supply chain capabilities to enable flexibility, agility, resilience and a new level of productivity in response to evolving global conditions.
