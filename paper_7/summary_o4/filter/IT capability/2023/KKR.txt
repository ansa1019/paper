The discussion and analysis repeatedly notes that general, administrative and other expenses “during the year ended December 31, 2023 compared to the year ended December 31, 2022” were driven in part by “a higher level of information technology and other administrative costs in connection with the overall growth of the firm,” and that “other operating expenses for the year ended December 31, 2023 compared to the prior period” likewise reflected “a higher level of information technology, occupancy, and other administrative costs in connection with the overall growth of the firm,” while for the “year ended December 31, 2022 compared to the year ended December 31, 2021,” the increase in general, administrative and other expenses was attributed in part to “professional fees, information technology and other administrative costs in connection with the growth of the firm.” These disclosures imply that KKR has been expanding its technology infrastructure, data management and administrative platforms to support its evolving coordination of assets, processes and interfirm transactions, enhance operational flexibility, improve information timeliness and reliability, and underpin its fund accounting, valuation, risk management and reporting capabilities in response to growth and changing business needs.
