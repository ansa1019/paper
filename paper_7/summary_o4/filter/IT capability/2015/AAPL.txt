In April 2015, the company announced a significant increase to its capital return program by raising the expected total size of the program to $200 billion through March 2017, including increasing its share repurchase authorization to $140 billion and raising its quarterly dividend, and during 2015 it spent $36.0 billion to repurchase shares and paid dividends and dividend equivalents of $11.6 billion while funding these actions through existing domestic cash, cash generated from ongoing U.S. operating activities, borrowings and the issuance of term debt denominated in multiple currencies; to preserve and optimize its financial flexibility Apple invests its marketable securities portfolio primarily in highly-rated, investment-grade securities with limits on credit exposure to any one issuer, regularly reviews the portfolio for other-than-temporary impairments, and employs interest rate and currency swaps to manage debt-related risks; the company continues to believe that focused investments in research and development are critical to its future growth and competitive position and are directly related to timely development of new and updated products central to its core business strategy; in response to competitive pressures and industry-wide global product pricing pressures, compressed product life cycles and potential increases in component costs and currency fluctuations, the company expects to take product pricing actions and manage its cost structures to maintain operating continuity and leverage fixed costs, while recognizing that gross margins may remain under downward pressure; operational resilience is supported by dynamic resource allocation through purchase commitments for components based on forecasted demand, accruals for estimated cancellation fees when orders are modified, and stringent reviews of manufacturing-related capital assets and inventory prepayments for impairment whenever events or circumstances indicate a carrying amount may not be recoverable; customer incentive programs and expected product returns are estimated and recorded in revenue reductions based on historical experience, with adjustments made if redemption rates or return levels differ from expectations; revenue from multi-element arrangements is allocated to deliverables using a hierarchy of vendor-specific objective evidence, third-party evidence and best estimates of selling price, with deferred recognition of unspecified software upgrade rights and non-software services amortized on a straight-line basis over the expected provision period; management’s critical accounting policies and estimates, including those for revenue recognition, marketable securities impairment, inventory valuation, warranty costs, uncertain tax positions and deferred tax assets, are based on historical experience and judgments about future conditions, are reviewed with the audit and finance committee, and are designed to preserve the integrity of the company’s performance measurement under both nominal and exceptional conditions; the company records provisions for probable losses in legal proceedings, indemnification obligations and other contingencies only when reasonably estimable, maintains directors and officers liability insurance to mitigate potential exposures, and regularly assesses its liquidity and contractual obligations to ensure readiness for unexpected events and adherence to its strategic objectives.
