Cisco designs and sells a broad range of technologies powering the internet since 1984, and across networking, security, collaboration, applications and the cloud its evolving intent-based technologies are constantly learning and adapting to provide customers with a highly secure, intelligent platform for their digital business. The company remains focused on accelerating innovation across its portfolio and has made continued progress on its strategic priorities, including the transition of its business model to increased software and subscription offerings and the growth of deferred revenue associated with recurring software and subscription offers. Cisco’s infrastructure platforms business—including Nexus 9000 data center switching, Catalyst 9000 campus switching, wireless products such as Meraki and Wave 2 Wi-Fi, hyperconverged data center offerings like HyperFlex and routing—along with its applications offerings covering unified communications, telepresence, conferencing, IoT from Jasper and analytics from AppDynamics, and its security portfolio of unified threat management, web security, policy and access and advanced threat products, underscores its capability to deploy and integrate physical and intangible IT resources to support customers’ evolving business needs. In its strategy and priorities, Cisco highlights that its customers are seeking intent-based networks delivering meaningful business value through automation, security and analytics across private, hybrid and multi-cloud environments, and it articulates its vision to deliver highly secure, software-defined, automated and intelligent platforms while accelerating its pace of innovation, increasing the value of the network and transforming its business model to meet dynamic market demands.
