We emphasize our evolving IT capability through initiatives such as increasing vehicle production and capacity, improving and developing battery technologies, enhancing our FSD capabilities and expanding our global infrastructure, and by focusing on products and services based on artificial intelligence, robotics and automation. Other features and services, including access to our supercharger network, internet connectivity and over-the-air software updates, are provisioned upon delivery of a vehicle and recognized over time, and our cost of automotive sales revenue includes vehicle connectivity costs and infrastructure allocations for the supercharger network, underscoring the integration of IT resources and operational efficiency. We continue to ramp production, build manufacturing capacity and expand operations to enable increased deliveries and deployments of our products and further revenue growth, which requires accurately projecting demand and infrastructure requirements globally and deploying production, workforce and other resources accordingly while working to prevent delivery capabilities from becoming a bottleneck and increasing the capability and efficiency of our servicing operations. In our energy business, we remain committed to growing retrofit solar energy by offering a low-cost and simplified online ordering experience and by improving installation capabilities and price efficiencies for solar roof through on-boarding and training new installers. Our selling, general and administrative expenses include personnel and facilities costs related to information technology, and we adapt our investment strategy to meet liquidity and risk objectives by investing in digital assets, including an aggregate $1.50 billion investment in bitcoin in the first quarter of 2021, managing our holdings based on business needs and market and environmental conditions.
