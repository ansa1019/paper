Tesla’s disclosures describe the integration and ongoing management of digital technologies and information systems as a core enabler of its business strategy and operational processes, noting in particular that features such as Autopilot, Full Self-Driving and over-the-air software updates are provisioned and recognized over time alongside internet connectivity and supercharger network access as stand-ready obligations; that vehicle connectivity costs and allocations to its supercharger network are included in cost of automotive sales and leasing; that the company continues to enhance its online ordering experience for solar energy and storage products through simplified digital interfaces; that management actively monitors macroeconomic conditions and demand forecasts to flexibly deploy production, workforce and infrastructure resources globally; and that selling, general and administrative expenses include personnel and facilities costs for its information technology organization, all of which underscore Tesla’s deployment of IT resources, connectivity, and information management capability in support of its competitive positioning and operational efficiency.
