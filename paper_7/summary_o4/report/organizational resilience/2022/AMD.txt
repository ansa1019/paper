In 2022, AMD completed its strategic acquisition of Xilinx, expanding its technology and product portfolio to include adaptable hardware platforms such as field programmable gate arrays, adaptive SoCs and the Versal ACAP VCK5000 development card designed to enable hardware acceleration and rapid innovation across diverse technologies and markets, and acquired Pensando to offer high-performance data processing units and a complementary software stack, together positioning AMD to provide the industry’s broadest set of leadership compute engines and accelerators that deliver optimized performance, security, flexibility and total cost of ownership for leading-edge data centers; execution of AMD’s product technology roadmap further introduced 4th Gen AMD EPYC™ processors with next-generation architecture, 3rd Gen AMD EPYC™ processors with 3D V-Cache technology, the AMD Instinct™ MI210 accelerator and ROCm™ 5 software—exascale-class technology tailored for HPC and AI workloads to reduce time to insights—and in the embedded segment, the Ryzen™ Embedded R2000 series SoCs and the Kria™ KR260 robotics starter kit to enable rapid development of hardware-accelerated applications for robotics, machine vision, IoT and industrial control; in client and professional markets, AMD introduced Ryzen™ 7000, 6000 and 5000 series desktop and mobile processors, a new AM5 chipset platform and multiple Radeon™ RX and Radeon Pro GPUs built on RDNA™ 2 and RDNA 3 architectures to enhance performance, energy efficiency and connectivity for gamers, creators and professionals; these investments, along with increased R&D spending, prepayments under long-term supply agreements and the integration and amortization of acquisition-related intangible assets, underscore AMD’s focus on mobilizing and deploying IT-based resources in combination with organizational capabilities to support business strategies, coordinate internal processes and interfirm relationships, and drive operational flexibility, innovation and competitive advantage.
