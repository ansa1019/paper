The company describes that its fleet support services in its global services segment encompass flight and maintenance training, field service support, engineering services, information services and systems and technical data and documents, and that the aerospace markets it serves include training and professional services as well as data analytics and digital services. It further reports that customers, notably airlines, are employing data analytics to plan flight operations and predictive maintenance to improve their productivity and efficiency and are outsourcing spares management to third parties to optimize inventory, reflecting an integrated technology-enabled coordination of internal processes, resources and interfirm relationships in support of business strategies. Additionally, the global services segment provides “innovative, comprehensive and cost-competitive product and service solutions” underpinned by digital capabilities to meet evolving market needs and deliver business value.
