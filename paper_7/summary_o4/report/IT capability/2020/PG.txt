Procter & Gamble’s disclosures emphasize its ability to successfully manage global financial risks, local, regional or global economic volatility and disruptions in credit markets to preserve financial flexibility; the company highlights its capacity to maintain key manufacturing and supply arrangements— including execution of supply chain optimizations, sole supplier and sole manufacturing plant arrangements—and to manage disruption of business due to factors outside its control, such as natural disasters, acts of war or terrorism and disease outbreaks; a multi-year productivity and cost-savings initiative that began in 2012, with specific elements communicated in 2017, drives ongoing outsourcing projects, supply chain and overhead optimization, workforce restructuring and manufacturing cost savings to sustain operating margins and resource deployment; global business services provides technology, processes and standard data tools to enable sector business units, enterprise markets and corporate functions to better understand the business and serve consumers and customers with minimal capital investment; the strategic focus on innovation—leveraging consumer insights, technology advancements and superior product, packaging, brand communication, retail execution and value equation—reinforces resilience by enabling the company to foresee, confront and benefit from sudden disruptive change; management’s disciplined approach to investing, even under significant macroeconomic pressures such as the COVID-19 pandemic, ensures the long-term health of the business is not sacrificed for short-term objectives; exposure to commodity price volatility is managed through pricing actions, cost savings projects, sourcing decisions and consistent productivity improvements; foreign exchange and changing government policies, including tax legislation and international trade agreements, are regularly assessed and managed to mitigate adverse impacts on sales, margins, earnings and cash flows; during the COVID-19 pandemic the company prioritized protecting the health and safety of employees, maximizing the availability of essential health, hygiene and cleaning products and deploying employee talents and resources to help society meet and overcome challenges, enabling the company to broadly maintain operations, work with government authorities to keep manufacturing facilities open, implement employee safety measures, manage supply chain disruptions and preserve liquidity with strong operating cash flow and access to capital markets; a supply chain finance program and extended payment terms with suppliers provide an operational hedge to support supplier liquidity while maintaining consistent payment obligations; robust liquidity and debt management practices—including committed credit facilities, strong short- and long-term credit ratings and disciplined capital allocation for dividends, share repurchases, acquisitions and capital expenditures—ensure financial resilience; and the use of derivative instruments and hedging activities to manage interest rate, currency exchange and commodity price volatility further underpins the enterprise’s ability to absorb strain and preserve or improve functioning under challenging conditions.
